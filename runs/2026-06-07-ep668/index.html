Theme analysis

1. RTX Spark and personal AI PC: strongest new EP668 signal

EP668’s best U.S.-tradable theme is NVIDIA’s attempt to make local AI PCs useful for creators, developers, gamers, and agentic workflows. The official NVIDIA release supports several important elements: RTX Spark is positioned for Windows-native personal agents, includes up to 128GB unified memory, and targets demanding creative/AI tasks locally. It also says MediaTek collaborated on the custom CPU design, which validates MediaTek as an important supply-chain signal but **not** a clean U.S. trading vehicle.

**Investment judgment:** NVDA remains a core watch, not a fresh chase. The theme is real enough to monitor product launch, OEM SKU pricing, attach rates, and whether local AI becomes incremental demand rather than just another spec cycle.

2. Adobe: contrarian upgrade from “AI victim” to workflow AI candidate

EP668 repeatedly highlights that Adobe appeared inside NVIDIA’s consumer AI PC story. This is important because the market often frames Adobe as threatened by generative AI. Adobe’s own March 2026 announcement with NVIDIA supports a different possibility: enterprise-grade creative, marketing, 3D, and agentic workflows may deepen Adobe’s platform role.

**Investment judgment:** upgrade ADBE to priority research. Still, do not confuse product relevance with stock attractiveness. ADBE needs evidence of growth reacceleration, monetization, retention, and margin durability.

3. Memory capacity and unified memory: MU becomes a cleaner U.S. monitor

EP668’s unified-memory emphasis points to a broader capacity question: local AI PCs and workstations may need much more memory to run larger models and multimodal workflows. The clean U.S.-listed memory monitor is **MU**, while many memory/component signals remain Asia-local.

**Investment judgment:** add MU to active watch, not a buy call. The next hard trigger is Micron’s planned FYQ3 2026 results on 2026-06-24; look for DRAM/HBM demand, high-capacity module pricing, inventories, and capex discipline.

4. AI PC competition: QCOM / AMD / INTC / ARM are monitors, not conclusions

EP668 suggests RTX Spark could challenge earlier AI PC attempts because the use case is more mature and the ROI argument is clearer. That creates a competitive-pressure dashboard: QCOM for Snapdragon/Windows-on-Arm, AMD and INTC for x86 AI PC response, and ARM for broader architecture economics.

**Investment judgment:** wait for Qualcomm Investor Day on 2026-06-24 and post-Computex OEM shipment evidence. Do not force a winner from a podcast signal.

5. Power rack / passive components: useful clues, still evidence-gated

EP668 discusses large power-rack components, LPU rack production, Kyber delays, backplanes, orthogonal structures, and Q-cloth. These are high-value research clues, but the concrete numbers remain unverified. For U.S.-tradable exposure, VSH is a liquid but imperfect proxy; MRAAY is thematically closer to Murata but OTC liquidity is a real constraint.

**Investment judgment:** evidence hunt only. Upgrade only with official company commentary, distributor lead-time/allocation evidence, or credible primary-source confirmation.
