2. TSMC after Q2: quality confirmed, entry still matters

TSMC remains the best-confirmed anchor in the current framework. The official Q2 release supports the AI/HPC foundry thesis, but the stock question is now valuation and reaction discipline. Keep TSM as 核心觀察, not an automatic add.

Upgrade only if a pullback or consolidation occurs without deterioration in AI/HPC demand, margin guidance, capex quality, or advanced-packaging signals. Downgrade if post-result price action combines with margin/capex disappointment or cautious AI demand language.

3. Liquidity/deleveraging overlay: price pressure is not always thesis failure

EP679's Korea/Taiwan leverage framework remains useful. If high-quality AI/semi names weaken while official company evidence stays strong, treat the first read as valuation/liquidity pressure rather than automatic fundamental deterioration. If official guidance weakens, then downgrade the thesis.

The practical portfolio rule is simple: in a deleveraging tape, prefer staged entries and smaller initial sizing over all-at-once conviction buys.

4. Power / analog / passive / OSAT: earnings should decide

TXN, STM, AMKR, and VSH are still proof gates, not chase candidates. Supply-chain price and lead-time stories can flag a setup, but upgrade requires official evidence: orders turning into revenue, gross margin, free cash flow, and guidance quality.

This keeps ON, ADI, VSH, AMKR, and ASX on the watchlist while avoiding narrative creep before the companies report.

5. AI application and NeoCloud: ROI proof over AI enthusiasm

For META, GOOGL, MSFT, ADBE, CRM, NOW, SNOW, and OKTA, the market-watch rule remains paid usage plus gross-margin resilience. For CRWV, the hurdle is higher because backlog, financing cost, customer concentration, utilization and pricing pressure can dominate headline growth.

META is the next dated application-layer check. The question is not whether Meta spends on AI; it is whether monetization and ad strength justify capex and depreciation.

6. Semicap and optical: require specific confirmation

ASML and optical/interconnect names can benefit from AI infrastructure strength, but TSMC strength does not validate every supplier equally. Keep ASML as priority research and optical names as higher-beta watches until order quality, backlog visibility, revenue and margin evidence improve.
