Theme analysis

1. TSMC: proof gate cleared, but entry discipline still matters

TSMC’s Q2 print is a clear positive. Official Q2 revenue reached US$40.20B, gross margin was 67.7%, and operating margin was 60.3%. Q3 revenue guidance of US$44.6B–45.8B implies continued sequential growth.

The reason this is not an automatic “buy now” signal is valuation and crowding. EP679’s liquidity lesson matters: when leverage is being removed from Asian markets, high-quality leaders can still pause or sell off if expectations were too high. The right label is 核心觀察 / confirmed quality, no chase.

2. EP679 liquidity overlay: forced selling is different from fundamental deterioration

EP679 frames Korea/Taiwan pressure as leverage and market microstructure rather than a simple semiconductor demand collapse. For a US-tradable portfolio, the practical implication is to tighten entry discipline around crowded AI/semi winners and avoid confusing short-term forced selling with thesis failure.

This supports a two-step process: first ask whether the selling is leverage/margin driven; then ask whether official company data is weakening. Today, TSM’s official data is strong, so any weakness would be treated first as valuation/liquidity risk unless future evidence changes.

3. AI application cost efficiency: useful AI is not always profitable AI

EP679’s AI discussion is important for software names. Agentic workflows can generate real productivity, but repeated verification, tool use, and domain-specific review can make inference/token cost high. That means AI application stocks should be judged by paid usage, gross-margin resilience, retention, and pricing power — not just by “AI feature” announcements.

US-tradable lane: META, GOOGL, MSFT, ADBE, CRM, NOW, SNOW, OKTA, and IGV. The strongest candidates are the ones that can turn AI usage into durable revenue without margin compression.

4. Power / IDM / OSAT: earnings verification is now the main path

EP679 reinforces the shift from chasing hot supply-chain narratives to waiting for financial confirmation. The next clean US-tradable checks are dated: TXN July 22, STM July 23, AMKR July 27, META July 29, and VSH August 5.

For ON, ADI, VSH, AMKR, and ASX, the upgrade condition remains the same: orders and utilization must convert into revenue, gross margin, and cash flow. Lead-time or price-hike stories are useful triggers, not proof.

5. Semicap and optical: TSM strength does not validate everything

TSM strength supports AI hardware demand, but it does not automatically validate every semicap, optical, or substrate name. ASML remains a priority monitor because the prior official message kept 2026 growth intact while leaving 2027 less certain. LITE, COHR, and AXTI remain high-beta optional watches until revenue and margin proof improves.
