Theme analysis

1. TSMC anchor: wait for July 10 / July 16

TSMC’s official 2026 monthly revenue page still shows June blank as of this run. January–May total revenue is NT$1,961.8B, +30.0% YoY. The financial calendar lists 2026-07-10 13:30 Asia/Taipei for June monthly revenue and 2026-07-16 14:00 Asia/Taipei for Q2 results/call.

Decision: TSM stays core watch. Do not front-run the print from supply-chain enthusiasm alone; require revenue plus margin/capex commentary and stock reaction.

2. OSAT / advanced packaging: AMKR date confirmed, ASX proof still pending

Amkor’s official IR page added a new 2026-07-07 release: Q2 results will be issued after Nasdaq close on 2026-07-27, with a 5:00 PM ET call. This makes AMKR the next clean US-listed OSAT proof point. In Q1, Amkor reported record Q1 sales of $1.685B, gross margin 14.2%, and Q2 guidance for $1.75–$1.85B sales with 14.5–15.5% gross margin.

The TSMC-Amkor 10-year Arizona advanced-packaging agreement supports the strategic relevance of AMKR, but it is a long-cycle capacity/collaboration signal. It does not prove near-term margin expansion by itself.

Decision: keep AMKR and ASX as priority research. Upgrade only if Q2 / monthly evidence shows margin and mix conversion, not just AI-packaging narrative.

3. Vishay / passive-discrete: best listed evidence, but watch capital structure

Vishay remains the cleanest listed proxy for passive/discrete conversion. Q1 2026 showed $839.2M revenue, 21.0% gross margin, book-to-bill 1.34 overall, 1.47 semiconductors and 1.23 passives, and management guided Q2 revenue to $875–905M with gross margin around 22.0% ±50 bps.

New since yesterday: Vishay announced that its 2.25% convertible senior notes due 2030 are convertible from 2026-07-05 through 2026-10-03 because the stock traded above the conversion threshold. This is not automatically bearish, but it means position sizing must consider potential dilution/cash-share settlement mechanics after the rally.

Decision: VSH remains priority research, but the upgrade test is now two-part: operating conversion plus manageable capital-structure impact.

4. ON / SYNA: event lane, not clean cycle exposure

The onsemi/Synaptics all-stock acquisition remains the most important ON item. It could broaden ON toward connected compute/control and physical AI, but it also turns ON into a deal-execution story.

Decision: ON stays priority research with event-risk label; SYNA is transaction-risk only. Do not read the deal as pure confirmation of power-semiconductor tightness.

5. Meta / CoreWeave NeoCloud: demand validation, not automatic buy signal

Meta’s official Q1 2026 report shows strong revenue and raised 2026 capex guidance to $125–145B. CoreWeave’s Q1 report showed $99.4B revenue backlog and multiple Meta agreements, including a $21B commitment. Together, they validate AI compute demand and capex intensity.

But the investment question is not “is demand real?”; it is whether demand converts into FCF, utilization, and durable returns. CRWV remains especially sensitive to financing, power availability, customer concentration, and valuation.

Decision: META stays a priority monitor for capex monetization; CRWV remains high-beta only.
