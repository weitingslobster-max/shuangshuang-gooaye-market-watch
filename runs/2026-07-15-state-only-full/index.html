Theme analysis

1. TSMC: demand evidence is strong; stock discipline depends on tomorrow's call

TSMC's June revenue already strengthened the AI/HPC foundry lane. But today adds no reason to front-run the July 16 Q2 event. The call needs to answer four questions: gross-margin quality, capex intensity, advanced-packaging bottlenecks, and whether AI demand language remains durable rather than overheated.

The disciplined action is not to chase the revenue headline. If the call confirms the thesis but the stock gaps hard, a patient entry still beats a forced buy.

2. ASML: useful caution signal for semicap breadth

ASML's Q2 release is not bearish by itself: Q2 sales and net income were healthy, and management still expects 2026 growth. The caution is the inability to confirm 2027 growth yet. For US-tradable investors, ASML is a clean ADR expression of advanced lithography, but the report argues for selectivity: AI foundry strength does not automatically mean every semicap name deserves multiple expansion now.

This read-through supports the existing framework: TSM can remain core quality while equipment/capex exposures should be monitored for order visibility, regional mix, and 2027 demand confidence.

3. Power / analog / discrete lane remains proof-before-price

ADI has the best official AI power-density support through the Empower acquisition. ON has event-driven optionality from the Synaptics transaction. VSH remains a passive/discrete recovery candidate.

But none of these has cleared the key burden of proof today. Price-letter, lead-frame, and lead-time claims stay as watchlist triggers until primary sources, distributors, or earnings calls confirm that they are converting into revenue, gross margin, and free cash flow.

4. OSAT lane: revenue is not enough

AMKR and ASX remain useful US-tradable OSAT expressions. The next upgrade condition is still margin conversion, not just revenue growth. Advanced packaging can be a strong story, but if utilization/capex intensity fails to lift gross margin and cash returns, the thesis remains incomplete.

5. AI compute-economics: Meta quality vs CoreWeave beta

META remains a higher-quality platform monitor for AI capex ROI. CRWV remains the direct but riskier NeoCloud expression. The investment split is unchanged: Meta needs monetization/ROI proof; CoreWeave needs backlog-to-cash conversion without financing stress, customer concentration deterioration, or pricing pressure.
