2. TSMC after Q2: quality confirmed, entry still matters

TSM remains the best-confirmed anchor in the current state: AI/HPC demand, advanced manufacturing importance, and foundry leadership are central to the framework. The stock question is now valuation and reaction discipline. A strong print can reduce fundamental uncertainty while increasing the risk of chasing after optimism is priced in.

Upgrade only if the market offers a pullback or consolidation without deterioration in AI/HPC demand, margin guidance, capex quality, or advanced-packaging signals. Downgrade if post-result behavior pairs with margin/capex disappointment, cautious demand language, or broad forced-selling pressure.

3. Liquidity/deleveraging overlay: keep position sizing humble

EP679's Korea/Taiwan deleveraging framework remains useful. Weakness in good AI/semi names should first be separated into two buckets: liquidity/valuation compression versus true company deterioration. That distinction matters because the right response differs: staged entries for liquidity stress, thesis downgrade for official guidance weakness.

4. Power / analog / passive / OSAT: earnings should decide

TXN, STM, AMKR, and VSH remain near-term proof gates. Supply-chain anecdotes around MOSFETs, PMICs, passive parts, OSAT and lead frames are useful alerts, but the investable proof must come from orders converting into revenue, gross margin, free cash flow and guidance.

This keeps ON, ADI, VSH, AMKR, and ASX on the watchlist while avoiding upgrades before company-level evidence arrives.

5. AI application and NeoCloud: ROI proof over enthusiasm

For META, GOOGL, MSFT, ADBE, CRM, NOW, SNOW, and OKTA, the key test is paid usage plus margin resilience. For CRWV, the bar is higher because financing cost, utilization, customer concentration and hyperscaler pricing can overwhelm backlog excitement.

META is the next dated application-layer check. The question is whether AI monetization and ad strength justify capex and depreciation, not whether AI spending is large.

6. Semicap and optical: do not over-extrapolate from TSM strength

ASML, LITE, COHR, MRVL, NOK, and AXTI can benefit from AI infrastructure, but they need their own proof. TSMC strength does not automatically validate every supplier, especially where order visibility, backlog mix, customer concentration, or margin conversion are uncertain.
