Theme analysis

1. Post-rally reset: EP674 turns risk control into the main operating rule

EP674 is not a fresh “buy list.” It is a framework for how to behave after a strong 4–5月 run and a sharp late-June reset. The podcast’s 60MA/seasonal-line warning translates well into US-market work: when a crowded AI or software leader breaks trend, the next rebound can meet trapped-buyer supply instead of clean accumulation.

Investment stance: reduce narrative weight, increase proof gates. Strong primary-source evidence still matters, but broken technical structure should keep sizing modest until revenue, margin and relative-strength proof reappear.

2. AI application-layer megacaps: GOOGL / MSFT / AAPL become more important

EP674’s most important new US-tradable bucket is the possible rotation from hardware buildout toward application-layer AI. Alphabet’s Q1 materials support the monitoring case because Google Cloud and AI infrastructure are now central to the company’s results and capex plan. Microsoft’s FY26 Q3 materials support the same debate from the opposite direction: Azure and Copilot demand are real, but AI infrastructure is pressuring cost of revenue and gross margin. Apple’s Q2 FY2026 results show broad revenue strength, but EP674’s Apple thesis still needs a more specific test: whether higher device pricing can hold without damaging shipments or upgrade cycles.

Research stance: priority research, not automatic add. GOOGL looks like the cleanest AI application/platform monitor; MSFT is the best enterprise workflow monitor but capex ROI scrutiny is high; AAPL is a pricing-power / edge-AI option that needs shipment and margin proof.

3. Micron / HBM: supplier proof plus customer-capex stress test

Micron’s FYQ3 2026 report is strong enough to keep MU in priority research. The important EP674 refinement is that high HBM/memory profitability can cut both ways. For MU, high HBM demand and margin are positive. For hyperscaler customers, high memory cost makes the AI-capex ROI question louder.

Research stance: priority research with cycle and customer-ROI discipline. Upgrade only if MU sustains HBM/data-center strength while GOOGL/MSFT/META/AMZN also show AI revenue, utilization or margin improvement sufficient to justify capex.

4. Passive, power and ABF: strong themes, weak pure US expression

EP674 keeps passive components, power components and IC substrates in the “relative strength / pricing” bucket, but the US expression remains imperfect. VSH is liquid but not a pure R-chip/passive price-hike vehicle. MRAAY is closer to Murata/passive but OTC/liquidity constrained. ON, MPWR, STM, TXN and UMC are cleaner US-tradable ways to watch power-tree / mature-node demand, but each needs product-specific proof.

Research stance: evidence-gated watch. Do not upgrade until price letters, distributor quotes, monthly revenue and margins move together for at least two months.

5. Host portfolio clues: SpaceX, Tesla, MRVL, CRWD

EP674 references SpaceX, Tesla, Marvell and CrowdStrike. For this workflow, SpaceX is not a normal public equity recommendation; any “SPCX” or other public access vehicle needs separate liquidity, structure and premium/discount analysis. TSLA is not upgraded; it is a funding-source / relative underperformance clue. MRVL and CRWD remain active US-tradable monitors, but EP674’s risk-control frame argues against chasing if trend and valuation are stretched.
