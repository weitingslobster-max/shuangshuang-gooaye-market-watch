Theme analysis

1. Passive components / MLCC / server power-chain squeeze

This remains the best evidence hunt but still not a clean buy signal. The thesis needs hard proof that AI-server component intensity is creating pricing power, not just better sentiment. VSH offers liquid US exposure to passives/discretes and remains useful for book-to-bill, backlog, margin, and order commentary. But it is not a pure high-capacitance MLCC/SP-Cap name. MRAAY is thematically closer to Murata and the MLCC chain, but OTC liquidity keeps it in tiny satellite / watch-only territory.

Upgrade path: official supplier price letters, distributor lead-time/allocation/stopped-order evidence, Murata/Vishay gross-margin follow-through, and broker-level proof that MRAAY spreads/dollar volume can support a tiny position.

2. SPCX / SpaceX and capital rotation

EP670’s space-capital-rotation theme remains active because a clean US expression changes the opportunity set. But clean access is not the same as attractive price. The risk is that early public-market excitement capitalizes several years of Starlink, Starship, defense, satellite, and AI-infrastructure optionality before investors have enough public-quarter data.

Keep SPCX = priority research / do not chase. Use RKLB and ASTS as relative-strength and independent-catalyst checks. If they hold up on their own milestones, they are not automatically invalidated by SpaceX leadership; if they keep underperforming without new proof, capital rotation back to the leader is a real warning.

3. Edge AI / model-control narrative

The Anthropic/Fable lane remains a narrative watch, not a forecast to buy every edge-AI name. For AAPL, QCOM, MU, ARM, AMD, and INTC, the proof points are concrete: device shipment attach rates, local inference usage, developer adoption, memory-content uplift, paid workflows, or margin improvement. The next hard tests are still Micron FYQ3 2026 and Qualcomm Investor Day on 2026-06-24.

4. TSM and AI infrastructure

TSM stays the cleanest US-tradable foundry watch. Monthly revenue strength and AI demand support the business-quality view, but the stock-upgrade gate is margin/pricing durability, capex discipline, and customer-demand breadth. For NVDA / AVGO / MRVL, the discipline is similar: official revenue/guidance can remain strong while valuation expectations are already demanding.

5. Software AI repair

The software repair lane remains plausible but valuation-sensitive. CRM, NOW, SNOW, OKTA, ADBE, and IGV only deserve upgrades if AI improves paid conversion, retention, workflow depth, ARR/RPO quality, and margins. Vague AI usage language is not enough; the report should keep demanding primary-source financial evidence.
