Theme analysis

1. TSMC remains the quality anchor, but quality is not a chase signal

The primary-source evidence remains constructive. TSMC’s June 2026 monthly revenue was NT$442.68B, up 67.9% YoY, and first-half 2026 revenue was NT$2.404T, up 35.6% YoY. The Q2 result page shows Q2 net revenue of US$40.20B, 67.7% gross margin, and Q3 revenue guidance of US$44.6–45.8B.

Investment interpretation: this supports the long-term AI/HPC foundry thesis, but EP679/EP680 still argue for no-chase behavior because crowded positioning can compress valuation even when fundamentals are strong.

2. Analog / mature-node recovery waits for TXN and STM

TXN reports after market on July 22 at 3:30 p.m. Central time. STM reports Q2 on July 23 and is still in its quiet period. Until those calls happen, mature-node recovery should remain a watchlist hypothesis, not a confirmed upcycle.

Upgrade evidence would be demand-led order recovery, utilization improvement and stable margins. Downgrade evidence would be recovery that looks like inventory restocking, cost-pass-through, or guidance that remains weak in industrial/auto.

3. OSAT / advanced packaging needs margin proof

AMKR will report Q2 after the Nasdaq close on July 27, followed by a 5:00 p.m. ET call. The current state’s OSAT thesis remains plausible because AI packaging/test demand is strategically important, but the investable proof is margin and guidance, not just revenue growth or customer-news excitement.

ASX remains a useful ADR monitor, while AMKR is the cleaner US-listed OSAT expression. Require gross-margin lift, advanced-package mix disclosure, and capex/customer evidence before upgrading.

4. Passive / discrete components need Vishay proof

VSH will release Q2 results before the NYSE opens on August 5, with a 9:00 a.m. ET call. This is the key US-listed proof gate for the passive/discrete theme because many local Asian passive-component names are not cleanly US-tradable.

Upgrade only if orders convert into revenue, margin and free cash flow. If price-letter and lead-time claims remain mostly secondary, keep MRAAY/MRAAF/ROHCY as industry signals with liquidity warnings.

5. AI efficiency and capex ROI waits for Meta and hyperscaler earnings

META will release Q2 results after market close on July 29 and hold the call at 1:30 p.m. Pacific. This is one of the cleanest official checks for the EP679/EP680 AI-efficiency framework: cheaper inference is bullish only if paid usage, ad/product monetization and capex productivity improve without gross-margin or depreciation pressure becoming the main story.

For META, GOOGL, MSFT, ADBE, CRM, NOW, SNOW and OKTA, keep requiring paid adoption, retention, margin and capex-ROI proof. Usage growth alone is not enough.
