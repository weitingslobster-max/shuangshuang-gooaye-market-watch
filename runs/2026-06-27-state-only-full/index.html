Theme analysis

1. QCOM / device-to-data-center AI: better roadmap, still proof-gated

Qualcomm's Investor Day 2026 is a meaningful update because it explicitly pushes the company beyond handset/edge AI into data-center strategy and broader compute-continuum ambitions. That supports the existing Gooaye state lane around edge AI, Physical AI and local-agent hardware. The weakness is that Investor Day is mostly a roadmap; the stock thesis should not be upgraded until the roadmap becomes measurable revenue, customer wins, margins and disclosure outside the handset cycle.

Research stance: priority research / no chase. QCOM gets more interesting if data-center and Physical AI convert into concrete revenue streams while handset/licensing remains stable. If future filings stay target-heavy and non-material, keep it as a watchlist name rather than an allocation idea.

2. MU / HBM and data-center memory: evidence-positive, cyclical discipline required

Micron reported record FYQ3 2026 results and framed memory as strategically important in the AI era, with HBM4E development underway and expected volume production in calendar 2027. That strengthens the dashboard's AI memory/HBM lane. The risk is classic memory-cycle behavior: pricing, inventory, customer capex digestion and supply discipline can change quickly.

Research stance: priority research, not automatic add. MU deserves active monitoring after FYQ3, but any position sizing should respect cyclicality and require follow-through in pricing, gross margin, inventory and HBM allocation.

3. AVGO / custom AI ASIC and networking: strongest confirmed custom-silicon anchor

Broadcom's Q2 FY2026 release remains one of the cleanest primary-source confirmations of the custom AI ASIC/networking lane. Management reported record revenue, operating profit and free cash flow, with accelerating AI semiconductor revenue. This supports AVGO as a dashboard anchor for Gooaye's custom AI silicon discussion.

The unresolved issue is specificity. EP673's Whitefish/wfish and ABF substrate angle is useful supply-chain intelligence, but not official product disclosure. Treat the package/ABF layer as a trigger, not a conclusion.

4. Optical/interconnect: official demand exists, but beta is high

LITE's FYQ3 2026 results showed record revenue and major margin expansion, with management tying growth to cloud photonics and future CPO/OCS growth drivers. NOK's Q1 2026 report showed Optical Networks up 20% and strong AI & Cloud demand. This keeps LITE, COHR, MRVL and NOK in active monitoring.

Research stance: active watch / no chase. Optical demand can be real and still punish late entries if supply, pricing, or hyperscaler inventory normalizes. Track orders, margins, capacity, customer concentration and backlog quality.

5. Power tree / passive / mature-node capacity: unchanged evidence gates

ON and MPWR provide cleaner US-tradable AI power evidence than most Taiwan/Japan local signals: onsemi said AI data-center revenue more than doubled year-over-year in Q1, and MPWR's Enterprise Data mix remains large. VSH offers a liquid passive/discrete proxy with supportive Q1 book-to-bill, but passive/R-chip remains less direct.

Research stance: keep the gates strict. Upgrade only if order strength converts into revenue, gross margin, free cash flow and explicit AI/server/power-tree exposure.
