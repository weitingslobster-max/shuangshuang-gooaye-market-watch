Theme analysis

1. TSMC: anchor intact; next proof is July 10 and July 16

TSMC’s own 2026 monthly revenue page shows May revenue of NT$416,975 million, up 30.1% YoY, and year-to-date revenue through May of NT$1,961,804 million, up 30.0% YoY. That keeps the AI/HPC foundry demand thesis alive. However, June revenue is still blank in the table at review time, and TSMC’s calendar points to July 10 for June monthly sales and July 16 for Q2 results.

Stance: 核心觀察 / wait for better entry. Upgrade only if June/Q2 data and guidance confirm AI/HPC demand, margin resilience, capex discipline and constructive post-event price/volume behavior.

2. Passive components: good Q1 setup, but still conversion-dependent

Vishay’s Q1 2026 release reported revenue of $839.2 million, gross margin of 21.0%, total book-to-bill of 1.34, semiconductor book-to-bill of 1.47, passive-component book-to-bill of 1.23, and backlog of 5.7 months. That is supportive for the passive/discrete dashboard, but it does not yet prove that July price letters are converting into durable earnings power.

Stance: 優先研究 / evidence-gated. VSH remains the cleaner liquid US proxy; MRAAY remains thematically closer but OTC-liquidity constrained. Require Q2 revenue, margins, backlog turns and free cash flow before promoting.

3. Power / MOSFET / mature-node: UMC adds signal, not conviction

UMC’s official monthly sales table shows May 2026 sales of NT$22,943,755 thousand, up 17.78% YoY, and 2026 YTD through May sales up 9.05%. That is directionally constructive for mature-node demand, but June was not yet populated at review time. A true upgrade still needs product-mix/utilization proof tied to power/PMIC/MOSFET rather than broad restocking.

Stance: 優先研究 / no chase. Continue tracking UMC, STM, ON, TXN, NXPI and MPWR, but demand product-specific margin and lead-time evidence.

4. ON + SYNA: strategically relevant, financially unproven

The official onsemi/Synaptics announcement says onsemi agreed to acquire Synaptics in an all-stock transaction with about $7 billion enterprise value and a fixed exchange ratio of 1.350 ON shares per SYNA share. The strategic rationale maps directly to EP675’s Physical-AI lane: power, sense, connected compute and control.

That makes ON/SYNA one of the cleanest US-tradable event monitors from the current state. It still does not turn ON into an automatic buy: all-stock M&A introduces dilution, shareholder/regulatory risk and integration execution risk, while ON’s auto/industrial cycle still matters.

5. AI application-layer and HBM ROI: keep as validation set

GOOGL, MSFT, AAPL and MU are not the direct EP675 component/power trade, but they validate whether the AI infrastructure capex stack can earn acceptable returns. If AI application monetization, cloud margin and HBM demand hold together, it supports TSM/AVGO/MRVL/MU/SMH breadth. If capex rises without revenue/margin proof, the whole hardware chain becomes more sell-the-news vulnerable.
