Theme analysis

1. TSMC after Q2: quality confirmed, stock discipline still unresolved

TSMC's official Q2 release remains the strongest primary-source fact in the current state. Revenue, gross margin and operating margin all confirmed high AI/HPC demand and strong execution. The next question is not whether TSMC is a good business; it is whether the stock offers a disciplined entry after a strong proof event.

For the report dashboard, this means TSM stays at 核心觀察. A pullback or consolidation with no deterioration in AI/HPC, margin, capex and advanced-packaging signals would be more attractive than chasing a post-result move.

2. EP679 liquidity overlay: do not confuse forced selling with thesis failure

EP679's Korea/Taiwan deleveraging framework is still useful as a market-structure lens. It does not automatically downgrade high-quality US-tradable names, but it does argue for smaller staged entries, cleaner stop/review rules, and patience around crowded AI/semi winners.

The key distinction for the next week is price pressure versus fundamental pressure. If price weakness appears without worse official guidance, treat it first as valuation/liquidity risk. If company guidance weakens, then downgrade the thesis.

3. AI application cost efficiency: usage must become profitable revenue

The AI application lane remains investable only where companies can show paid adoption and economic durability. META, GOOGL, MSFT, ADBE, CRM, NOW, SNOW, and OKTA should not be upgraded for AI activity alone. The proof is revenue quality, retention, gross margin and capex/depreciation productivity.

META is the most immediate dated test because Q2 is scheduled for July 29. For CRWV, the bar is even higher: backlog and demand need to convert into cash economics without financing stress or hyperscaler pricing pressure.

4. Power / analog / passive / OSAT: official earnings should decide the next move

The next clean US-tradable checks are TXN, STM, AMKR, META, and VSH. Until these results arrive, supply-chain price and lead-time stories are only triggers. The upgrade standard is still concrete: orders convert into revenue, gross margin and free cash flow.

This keeps ON, ADI, VSH, AMKR, and ASX on priority watch while preventing narrative creep. TXN and STM are especially important because they can tell us whether the mature-node/power recovery is demand-led or still mostly inventory and cost pass-through.

5. Semicap and optical: stay selective

ASML and optical/interconnect names can benefit from AI infrastructure strength, but TSMC strength does not validate every supplier equally. Keep ASML as a priority monitor, but require orders/backlog and 2027 visibility. Keep LITE, COHR, AXTI, MRVL, and related optical names as higher-beta watches until official revenue and margin proof improves.
