Theme analysis

AI-server power / MOSFET / PMIC

The core thesis remains intact but not fully proven. onsemi is the clearest public source because its Q1 materials said AI data-center revenue more than doubled year over year and grew more than 30% sequentially, with adoption across the power tree. Its June 9 GaNEXUS announcement reinforces the same direction: higher density and thermal efficiency for AI data centers and adjacent power applications.

MPWR remains the most direct PMIC/server-power expression. Its Q1 2026 commentary showed Enterprise Data revenue of $262.8M, up 97.7% year over year, driven by power-management solutions for AI and server applications. That is strong business evidence, but the stock still needs valuation discipline and evidence that growth is broadening rather than becoming too customer- or platform-concentrated.

What is still missing: hard public confirmation that specific server MOSFET/PMIC/high-voltage parts are broadly in allocation, that lead times have moved into extreme ranges across multiple suppliers/customers, and that price increases are converting into margins. Without that, this remains priority research, not a buy signal.

STM / IDM power-semiconductor follow-through

STM stays on the priority list because its June 2026 release raised 2026 data-center revenue ambition to about $1B. That is a meaningful AI-infrastructure signal and a clean NYSE-tradable expression. The caution is that a data-center revenue target does not automatically prove the EP672 shortage/pricing claim. The next checks are order conversion, gross-margin durability, and explicit power/AI infrastructure content in the next earnings materials.

UMC / mature-node capacity

UMC's May 2026 sales were NT$22.94B, up 17.78% year over year, and Jan-May sales were up 9.05%. That supports the idea that mature-node demand is improving, but it does not identify product mix. For this thesis, UMC should be treated as a capacity/utilization monitor until management or filings show power/PMIC/MOSFET mix and margin conversion.

Passive/discrete proxy check

Vishay remains useful because it is liquid and US-listed, but it is not a pure MLCC/SP-Cap or AI-server PMIC expression. Q1 book-to-bill of 1.34, with 1.47 for semiconductors and 1.23 for passives, is constructive. The gate is conversion: backlog turns, revenue, gross margin and free cash flow need to follow before upgrading the proxy.

QCOM and MU dated catalysts

Qualcomm's Investor Day is later today, so the discipline is to wait for actual materials. Upgrade QCOM only if data-center / Physical AI economics become material and measurable rather than optional narrative. Micron reports FYQ3 later today; the useful read-through will be HBM/DRAM pricing, inventory, capex discipline, and whether AI memory strength broadens without overheated expectations.
