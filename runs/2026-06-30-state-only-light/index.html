Material primary-source check

AI application layer: keep priority, require monetization proof

  • MSFT remains the cleanest enterprise AI workflow monitor. Microsoft reported FY26 Q3 revenue of $82.9B, up 18%, Intelligent Cloud revenue of $34.7B, up 30%, Azure and other cloud services revenue growth of 40%, and AI annual revenue run-rate above $37B. That supports the AI demand thesis, but the investment gate remains capex/FCF discipline rather than feature momentum alone.
  • GOOGL remains a priority platform monitor, but this light run did not find a new official event after the prior state update that would justify changing stance. The next hard gate is still Cloud growth, Gemini monetization, search/ads durability, and capex return proof from Alphabet IR materials.
  • AAPL remains watch-only for edge-AI/device pricing power. Apple IR still points investors to FY26 Q2 results and SEC filings plus June Newsroom product announcements. The upgrade gate is not WWDC/product language; it is device demand, mix, gross margin, and AI features that support upgrade cycles without discounting.

MU/HBM and hyperscaler capex ROI: still priority, still cyclical

Micron remains the most recent high-signal primary-source catalyst already captured by the state. Its FYQ3 2026 results and Form 10-Q keep the HBM/data-center memory thesis alive, but the same caution applies: supplier strength can be positive for MU while increasing cost pressure for hyperscaler customers. The dashboard should continue pairing MU evidence with MSFT/GOOGL/META/AMZN capex ROI commentary.

AVGO/MRVL custom silicon and networking: evidence is strong, valuation still matters

  • AVGO remains a priority research name. Broadcom's Q2 FY2026 release reported accelerating AI semiconductor revenue and guided further AI semiconductor growth. That supports the custom accelerator/networking lane, but the Whitefish/ABF package-intensity details still need source-backed confirmation before treating substrate suppliers as direct beneficiaries.
  • MRVL remains a priority but high-beta monitor. Marvell reported Q1 FY2027 net revenue of $2.418B, up 28% year over year, data center revenue of $1.833B, and guided Q2 revenue to $2.7B midpoint. This reinforces AI/custom/optical demand; it does not remove valuation and customer-concentration risk.

Passive, power and mature-node: no upgrade without conversion

The passive/R-chip, AI power-tree and mature-node lanes remain important but proof-gated:

  • VSH is still the liquid US proxy for passive/discrete conversion, but needs book-to-bill, price, margin and FCF proof.
  • MRAAY/MRAAF stay signal/small-satellite only because OTC liquidity and spread risk matter.
  • MPWR/ON/STM/TXN/UMC/ADI need product-specific AI power, PMIC, MOSFET or utilization evidence. Generic semiconductor recovery wording is not enough.
