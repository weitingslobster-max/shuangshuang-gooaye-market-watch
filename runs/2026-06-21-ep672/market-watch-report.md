## Theme analysis

### AI-server power / MOSFET / PMIC

EP672 strengthens the thesis that AI-server power architecture is stressing mature power components, not just leading-edge compute. The episode’s most valuable claim is also the least externally verified: 99+ week lead times and second-source transfers. Primary-source evidence supports the broad backdrop — TI grew Q1 revenue with industrial and data-center strength, onsemi reported AI data-center revenue more than doubled year over year, and STMicroelectronics described demand improvement and datacenter revenue targets. That is enough to keep the theme high priority, but not enough to call the shortage/price-hike thesis fully verified.

### Mature-node and 6-inch/8-inch capacity

EP672 makes capacity access central: if customers cannot get enough from primary IDMs, second-source qualification and foundry capacity matter. **UMC** is the cleanest US-tradable ADR for this lane, but the evidence gate is strict: overall utilization improvement is not enough. We need product-mix proof that power/PMIC/high-voltage MOSFET demand is driving utilization and margins, not display driver, networking, or generic restocking.

### Consumer/auto weakness as a cycle “speed limiter”

The episode frames weak consumer and automotive demand as a possible speed limiter that keeps the AI cycle from overheating too quickly. Treat this as a useful hypothesis, not a proven macro fact. If auto/consumer demand suddenly recovers, it may tighten supply further but also raise cost and demand-destruction risk.

### Robotics and biotech

Robotics stays in the parking lot until revenue, orders, or production milestones improve. Biotech in EP672 is more of a trading-psychology warning than an investable US-market theme.
