Theme analysis

1. Passive components: demand-driven pricing claim is stronger, but evidence gate remains strict

EP666 reinforces the recurring Gooaye signal that passive components may be moving from cost-driven price hikes to demand-driven tightness. The key products are aluminum capacitors, SP-Cap / OS-CON-like polymer capacitors, high-end MLCC, inductors, and resistors.

US-tradable translation:

  • MRAAY remains the closest Murata/MLCC expression, but its OTC nature makes it a *small satellite only* candidate.
  • VSH remains the best liquid U.S. proxy. Vishay’s official Q1 2026 materials show improving order intake, including a passive-components book-to-bill above 1, which is exactly the kind of primary evidence this workflow wants. But VSH is broad and not a pure MLCC/SP-Cap play.
  • Taiwan/Japan/China local companies remain industry signals only unless a clean and liquid U.S. expression appears.

Evidence needed before upgrade: official price letters, distributor lead-time/allocation data, company commentary that links tightness to AI server / data-center demand, and margin follow-through.

2. Software / SaaS AI repair: promoted to priority research

EP666’s most investable U.S. addition is the software repair thesis. Primary-source checks support the broad direction:

  • Salesforce reported Q1 FY2027 with cRPO growth and management pointing to Sales, Service, Slack, Agentforce, and Data 360 as drivers of second-half acceleration.
  • ServiceNow Q1 2026 official results showed subscription revenue growth and continued positioning around AI workflow automation.
  • Snowflake Q1 FY2027 official filing/release showed strong product-revenue growth, supporting the idea that AI can increase data-cloud workload rather than simply destroy software value.
  • Okta Q1 FY2027 gives a security/identity angle to enterprise AI adoption; every AI workflow still needs identity, governance, and access control.
  • IGV becomes the clean ETF expression if single-name valuation risk is too high.

The danger is valuation. Software rerating can move faster than revenue conversion. The upgrade is to priority research, not “buy any breakout.”

3. AI application moat: data + workflow + accountability

EP666’s framework is useful: when models commoditize, enterprise value may sit with whoever owns proprietary data, embeds deeply in workflow, and can take responsibility for business outcomes. That points toward software platforms, data platforms, identity/security, systems integrators, and vertical SaaS.

For public-market work, the measurable indicators are: remaining performance obligation, subscription/product revenue growth, net retention, usage-based billing, AI SKU adoption, margin impact, and customer-concentration risk.

4. Hardware / old AI: keep watching, tighten entries

NVDA and AVGO still have very strong official evidence, but EP666’s market-high / leverage warning says the entry bar must be higher. NVIDIA’s latest official Q1 FY2027 results show record revenue and data-center strength; Broadcom’s next Q2 FY2026 print on 2026-06-03 is the key near-term event for custom AI accelerator/networking validation.

TSM remains the foundry bellwether; TXN and NXPI remain analog/mature-node recovery checks. QCOM remains an optional watch until Investor Day gives more evidence.

5. Market structure: do not let rotation become leverage

EP666 highlights a market that can be fundamentally healthy while still fragile because of leverage, high prices, and liquidity constraints. For this workflow, that means:

  • No market orders in thin OTC lines.
  • No leverage-based recommendation from secondary notes.
  • No forced upgrade just because a theme is moving.
  • Prefer ETFs or liquid large caps when evidence is broad but ticker mapping is weak.
