Theme analysis

1. Passive components / R-chip: strongest new EP673 signal, weakest clean US expression

EP673’s clearest source-layer signal is that R-chip/passive components may be moving into a price-up cycle, with July price action and procurement behavior as the next proof points. The investment issue is expression quality. The cleanest operating assets are largely Taiwan/Japan local lines; for a US-only portfolio, VSH is liquid but imperfect, while MRAAY is thematically better but OTC/liquidity constrained.

Research stance: upgrade the evidence hunt, not the stock recommendation. The right dashboard is July revenue, price letters/distributor quotes, lead times, order cancellations, and gross-margin commentary. If those move together for two months, the cycle can graduate from “interesting” to “investable.”

2. Broadcom / Whitefish / ABF: AVGO remains the cleanest US-tradable anchor

EP673’s Broadcom wfish/Whitefish discussion points toward custom AI silicon packaging and ABF substrate intensity. Official Broadcom Q2 FY2026 results already support the broader thesis: AI semiconductor revenue accelerated materially and management guided Q3 revenue higher. The narrower package/ABF supplier split is not official enough to underwrite local supplier conclusions, but it strengthens the watch on AI custom silicon breadth.

Research stance: AVGO priority research / no chase. If Broadcom keeps beating AI revenue while supply-chain evidence confirms larger package/substrate content, AVGO remains a core proof point. But expectations already embed a lot of AI growth, so position discipline matters more than narrative excitement.

3. Optical / interconnect spread: move LITE, COHR, NOK into active monitoring

EP673’s QA layer mentions optical names. Recent official results justify taking the lane seriously: LITE and COHR reported strong AI/data-center-related demand, while NOK reported Q1 Optical Networks growth and AI & Cloud order strength. This is a valid monitoring lane, but high-beta optics can punish late entries when supply catches up or a hyperscaler digests inventory.

Research stance: LITE/COHR/NOK are active monitors, not automatic buys. Require sustained orders, margin expansion, supply execution and customer diversification. MRVL remains the higher-priority semiconductor interconnect name from earlier Gooaye work.

4. Power components / 8-inch capacity: reinforced, not changed

EP673 reiterates that power-device winners need real mature-node/8-inch capacity and back-end capability. That fits the existing dashboard: ON, MPWR, STM, TXN, UMC and VSH. The framework is useful because it filters out “power semiconductor” label-chasing. The investable question is capacity control plus demand conversion.

Research stance: keep the same gates — lead times, allocation, price increases, utilization, product mix, and margin conversion. No evidence, no upgrade.

5. Market liquidity / Korea leverage: risk framework only

EP673 discusses market pullback and possible Korea/high-leverage spillover risk. This is a useful risk-control prompt, but not a proven causal model. Treat it as a reason to avoid chasing hot lanes after vertical moves, not as a signal to abandon the AI infrastructure dashboard.
