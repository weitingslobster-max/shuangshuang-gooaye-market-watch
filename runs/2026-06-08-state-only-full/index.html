Theme analysis

1. Workflow AI repair: ADBE plus software dashboard

EP668 made this lane more interesting because workflow AI is easier to underwrite when it saves professional time, expands usage, or protects retention. ADBE is the most direct creative-workflow expression. CRM/NOW/SNOW/OKTA remain enterprise workflow/data/identity dashboard names, and IGV remains the diversified expression if single-name risk is too high.

Investment judgment: ADBE remains priority research, not a chase. Upgrade only if official earnings/product disclosures show AI monetization, retention, seat expansion, usage growth, or margin protection.

2. Local AI PC / workstation hardware and memory capacity

NVDA remains the cleanest benchmark for local AI hardware; MU is the memory-capacity/HBM monitor. The thesis is plausible: more local models and professional AI workflows may pull more GPU, memory, and high-capacity system demand. The risk is that enthusiasm runs ahead of revenue attribution.

Investment judgment: keep NVDA no-chase and MU priority research into FYQ3. Upgrade MU only if DRAM/HBM/high-capacity demand and pricing are visible in official guidance.

3. AI interconnect, optical, and custom silicon

AVGO and MRVL remain the strongest EP667/EP668 hardware-spread names, alongside NVDA and TSM. Primary-source evidence supports AI networking/custom silicon demand, but the market already knows this theme. Position sizing and entry discipline matter more than narrative strength.

Investment judgment: priority research; no chase. Require durable AI revenue, margin quality, and customer concentration disclosure.

4. AI PC competition: QCOM, AMD, INTC, ARM

QCOM Investor Day on 2026-06-24 is the cleanest scheduled catalyst. AMD, INTC, and ARM stay useful architecture monitors, but none receives an automatic upgrade from the EP668 workflow/local-AI-PC signal.

Investment judgment: monitor only until official revenue paths and OEM attach evidence are clearer.

5. Power/analog and passive components

ON/ADI/TXN/NXPI stay recovery watches, but the right question is demand durability, not simple restocking. VSH/MRAAY remain the U.S.-tradable passive-component expressions, but the shortage/price-hike thesis still needs official price letters, distributor lead-time/allocation evidence, or supplier margin follow-through.

Investment judgment: keep the evidence hunt; do not promote OTC or local Asian tickers without tradability and primary evidence.
