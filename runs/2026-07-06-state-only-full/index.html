Theme analysis

1. OSAT / advanced packaging: strongest new US-tradable lane, still margin-gated

The OSAT thesis has the cleanest US-tradable translation from EP676: ASX for ASE Technology and AMKR for Amkor. Primary-source evidence remains constructive but not conclusive.

  • Amkor reported Q1 2026 net sales of $1.685 billion, up 27% year over year, with gross margin of 14.2%. The company also described progress on advanced-packaging customer programs and factory utilization.
  • TSMC and Amkor announced a 10-year Arizona advanced-packaging/testing partnership on June 16, 2026. That validates advanced packaging as strategically important, but a long-term capacity agreement is not the same as near-term margin proof.
  • ASE’s latest monthly revenue release available before this run was May 2026, published June 9. It showed strong year-over-year revenue growth and keeps ASX on the priority list, but June/Q2 data are still needed for a fresh upgrade.

Decision: ASX and AMKR stay 優先研究 / no chase. The upgrade trigger is ATM/advanced-packaging growth converting into margin expansion and constructive Q2/Q3 guidance.

2. Power / MOSFET / passive components: good order signals, incomplete pricing proof

EP676’s strongest industry claim is that AI-server demand can crowd out power/MOSFET/passive capacity and create pricing power. The public proof is not complete yet.

  • Vishay’s Q1 2026 release is useful because book-to-bill was 1.34 overall, 1.47 for semiconductors and 1.23 for passive components. That supports stronger orders, especially for the power/passive dashboard.
  • But VSH still needs to convert orders into revenue, gross margin and free cash flow. A high book-to-bill can also precede over-ordering if customers fear shortages.
  • onsemi’s all-stock Synaptics acquisition gives ON a concrete Physical-AI event path across power, sensing, compute and control, but it also adds integration, dilution and timing risk. The announced closing target is mid-2027, not an immediate earnings catalyst.

Decision: ON, VSH, STM, MPWR, UMC, TXN and ADI remain the proof basket. Do not upgrade the whole basket until lead-time, ASP, allocation and margin evidence appear in multiple primary/channel sources.

3. TSMC: the anchor event is still ahead

TSM remains the best single anchor for the AI hardware chain, including advanced packaging. But the calendar matters: the decisive checks are still upcoming.

  • TSMC’s official financial calendar lists the Q2 2026 earnings conference for July 16, 2026.
  • TSMC’s monthly revenue page is the right source for the July 10 June-sales check.
  • The market-watch should compare TSMC’s AI/HPC and advanced-packaging commentary against ASX/AMKR/ON/VSH follow-through. If TSMC is strong but downstream margin proof fails, EP676’s spillover thesis weakens.

Decision: TSM stays 核心觀察 / wait for better entry. No pre-event chase.

4. META / CRWV: compute demand validation with asymmetric risk

META and CRWV remain a useful validation pair for the NeoCloud / capex-monetization lane, but they should not be collapsed into the same risk bucket.

  • Meta’s Q1 2026 release raised 2026 capex guidance to $125–145 billion including finance lease principal payments. That supports the “still investing aggressively in AI infrastructure” interpretation.
  • CoreWeave remains the high-beta public AI-cloud read-through. The useful question is not whether demand exists; it is whether backlog converts to profitable revenue without financing, power, utilization or customer-concentration stress.

Decision: META is a priority AI-capex/ROI monitor. CRWV is a high-beta monitor, not a core holding unless separate position sizing and financing risk work is done.

5. Monday cash-market trigger: prepare, then verify

Because this report is generated at 06:00 Pacific, before the July 6 US cash open, it should not assert that Monday breadth confirmed EP676. The check is:

  • Do ASX, AMKR, ON, VSH and TSM confirm with volume and breadth?
  • Is the rally broad across AI spillover names, or does it narrow back into crowded megacap AI only?
  • Are moves supported by company-specific news, or just pre-earnings positioning?

Decision: treat Monday tape as a trigger, not as evidence already observed.
