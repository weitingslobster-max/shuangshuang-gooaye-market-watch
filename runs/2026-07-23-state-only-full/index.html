Theme analysis

1. Analog / mature-node recovery has better official proof after TI

Texas Instruments reported Q2 revenue of $5.46B, net income of $1.98B and EPS of $2.14. Management said revenue increased 13% sequentially and 23% YoY, with broad growth led by industrial, data center and automotive. Q3 guidance is $5.65B–$6.15B revenue and $2.23–$2.57 EPS.

Investment interpretation: this clears the immediate “wait for official proof” hurdle for TXN. It does not mean buy-any-price. The better conclusion is that analog/mature-node recovery probability increased; the entry discipline is to wait for price reaction, margin durability and confirmation from peers (ADI, NXPI, STM, ON).

2. STM strengthens the power/IDM and AI-datacenter demand lane

STMicroelectronics reported Q2 revenue of $3.49B, gross margin of 34.8%, operating income of $187M and diluted EPS of $0.24. The more important update is forward-looking: management cited strong bookings in all end markets, improved visibility, tight supply in several categories, and distribution inventory below normal target. Q3 midpoint guidance is $3.70B revenue and 37.0% gross margin, with Q4 revenue expected above $4B.

ST also raised datacenter ambition to above $1B revenue in 2026 and well above $2B in 2027. That gives the EP672/EP679 power-semiconductor thesis a cleaner US-tradable ADR expression through STM, but margin and cash conversion still determine stock attractiveness.

3. Alphabet gives real AI monetization proof, but capex funding risk rises

Alphabet Q2 revenue grew 24% to $119.8B. Google Services grew 15% to $94.5B, and Google Cloud revenue grew 82% to $24.8B. Cloud operating income rose to $8.8B, a strong sign that AI infrastructure and AI solutions can generate revenue and margin together.

This supports the Gooaye AI-efficiency/Jevons framework: lower AI cost and wider AI usage can be investable when paid usage, revenue and margin all appear. The caveat is important: Alphabet also disclosed a $49.6B equity capital raise for AI infrastructure and global compute, plus a potential $40B ATM program primarily for tax obligations tied to employee equity grants. That makes capex productivity and dilution governance a key risk monitor.

4. TSMC remains quality anchor; do not confuse proof with entry

No new TSMC primary data changed today’s state. The prior official evidence remains strong: Q2 revenue, gross margin, AI/HPC demand and Q3 guidance supported the quality anchor thesis. But the market-watch discipline stays the same: even great fundamentals can suffer from crowding, valuation compression or short-term deleveraging.

Use TSM as a core watch and SMH/SOXX as liquid breadth expressions when single-name entries are unattractive.

5. Advanced packaging still waits for Amkor margin proof

The TSMC-Amkor 10-year Arizona agreement confirms strategic direction for U.S. advanced packaging and test capacity. But it is not yet a P&L upgrade. AMKR reports July 27 after the Nasdaq close; the key question is whether advanced packaging and test demand converts into gross margin, guidance and cash flow.

ASX remains a useful ADR monitor, but the same rule applies: revenue-only strength is not enough.

6. Passive/discrete components remain evidence-hunt, not confirmed upgrade

VSH is still the cleanest US-listed passive/discrete checkpoint, with Q2 on August 5. MRAAY, MRAAF, and ROHCY remain industry signals because OTC liquidity and spreads can dominate the economics. Upgrade only if official orders, margin, free cash flow and guidance improve.
