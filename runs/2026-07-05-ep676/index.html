Theme analysis

1. OSAT / advanced packaging: new high-priority lane, but not yet a valuation green light

EP676’s most actionable new US translation is OSAT. ASE and Amkor are both clean US-tradable expressions, unlike many Taiwan local packaging/test names.

Primary-source support is constructive:

  • ASE Technology reported May 2026 consolidated net revenue of NT$63.033 billion, up 28.6% YoY, and ATM assembly/testing/material revenue of NT$42.162 billion, up 37.9% YoY.
  • ASE’s Q1 2026 release showed consolidated net revenue up 17.2% YoY and EPS of NT$3.24 / US$0.205 per ADS.
  • Amkor’s Q1 2026 release and investor materials describe advanced-packaging programs and AI/HPC-related ramps, while press releases also point to a TSMC-Amkor US advanced-packaging partnership.

The upgrade condition is not “revenue up.” The upgrade condition is evidence that AI packaging/test capacity is improving pricing, mix and margin enough to offset normal smartphone/auto/consumer cyclicality. Until Q2 and June data confirm that, ASX/AMKR are priority research, not chase.

2. Power / MOSFET / passive components: strongest EP676 industry signal, weakest clean single-name purity

EP676 reinforces the power/MOSFET and passive-component shortage/pricing theme, but many named local companies do not have clean US-tradable access. For Weiting’s US account, the disciplined expression remains a basket of proof names:

  • ON / STM / VSH for power, discrete and passive/discrete read-throughs.
  • MPWR / ADI / TXN / NXPI for higher-quality power/analog recovery proof.
  • UMC for mature-node capacity/utilization signal.
  • MRAAY only as a small, liquidity-constrained passive/MLCC satellite.

Vishay’s Q1 2026 data remains supportive: revenue $839.2 million, gross margin 21.0%, total book-to-bill 1.34, passive book-to-bill 1.23 and backlog 5.7 months. That is a good dashboard input, but still not sufficient proof that July price-letter stories will become durable earnings.

3. Meta / NeoCloud: capex monetization is plausible, but not yet modelable revenue

EP676’s Meta point is important because it reframes compute rental as a way to keep investing in AI, not as a retreat. Official sources support the spending backdrop: Meta’s Q1 2026 release guided full-year 2026 capex to $125–145 billion, mostly for servers, data centers and network infrastructure. That is consistent with “doubling down.”

CoreWeave is the clean public NeoCloud comparable. Its Q1 2026 release reported revenue backlog of $99.4 billion and multiple Meta agreements, including a $21 billion commitment signed in March. That supports demand for rented AI compute, but it also raises two risks:

1. If Meta becomes both a major customer and a future compute supplier, CRWV’s strategic position may be less one-directional than the backlog headline suggests. 2. Heavy infrastructure buildout creates financing, power-supply and utilization risk if AI demand pauses.

Stance: META is a priority AI-capex/ROI monitor; CRWV is a high-beta NeoCloud monitor. Neither is upgraded from this report alone.

4. TSMC remains the anchor; EP676 adds downstream confirmation routes

TSM remains the core AI hardware anchor, but the next proof is still ahead. TSMC’s financial calendar points to June monthly sales on July 10, 2026 and Q2 2026 results/call on July 16. EP676 adds downstream confirmation routes — OSAT, passive, power, mature-node utilization — that should be checked against TSMC’s AI/HPC and advanced-packaging commentary.

Stance: 核心觀察 / wait for better entry. If TSMC data is strong but downstream margins fail to improve, EP676’s spillover thesis weakens.

5. Market rotation: strength can continue, but weekend evidence is not enough

EP676 discusses the possibility that strong groups re-assert leadership after a minor correction. Because this run occurs on Sunday, there is no live cash-market confirmation. Monday 2026-07-06 breadth, volume and relative strength matter more than weekend narratives.

The correct posture is to prepare the dashboard, not front-run it.
