Theme analysis

1. AI-server power / discrete / PMIC — EP671’s best US-tradable lane

EP671’s most investable US expression is not Taiwan local power/discrete names; it is the US-listed power-management and power-tree group. MPWR is the cleanest addition: official Q1 materials show first-quarter revenue of $804.2m, Enterprise Data revenue of $262.8m, and management attributed the sequential Enterprise Data increase to higher sales of power-management solutions for AI and server applications. This maps tightly to EP671’s PMIC / server power-density theme.

ON also deserves a higher priority. Its Q1 release said AI data-center revenue more than doubled year over year, accelerated more than 30% sequentially, and was driven by broader adoption across the power tree with multiple chip vendors and hyperscalers. That is direct enough to upgrade ON from broad power-semiconductor watch to priority research.

The caveat: both stocks can be right businesses at the wrong price. The report should demand continued AI data-center revenue, gross-margin durability, customer breadth, and guidance follow-through before any buy/add upgrade.

2. TXN / analog recovery — good company, less pure EP671 expression

TXN reported Q1 2026 revenue of $4.825b, up 19% year over year, with EPS of $1.68. This supports the analog/industrial-cycle recovery lane, and TXN remains a core watch. But EP671’s high-conviction power-tree thesis needs product-level server/AI proof; TXN is too broad to treat as a direct beneficiary without more specific data-center PMIC/power commentary.

3. VSH / passive and discrete proxy — evidence is improving, but not pure MLCC

VSH remains useful because it is liquid and directly spans discrete semiconductors and passive components. Its Q1 2026 book-to-bill was 1.34 overall, 1.47 for semiconductors, and 1.23 for passives, with backlog at 5.7 months and Q2 revenue guidance above Q1. That supports the “cycle is improving” part of EP671.

But it still does not prove the strongest MLCC/SP-Cap squeeze claims. Keep VSH = active liquid proxy, while MRAAY = closer thematic Murata/MLCC expression but OTC liquidity-gated. Do not let crowded passive-component sentiment become an automatic chase signal.

4. TSM as defensive/core reallocation — strong revenue, fund-flow proof still missing

TSMC’s official May revenue was NT$416.98b, up 1.5% month over month and 30.1% year over year; January-May revenue was up 30.0% year over year. This supports business quality and AI demand, but EP671’s “funds return to TSMC” setup is a flow thesis, not simply a revenue thesis. The next proof points are actual fund/ETF weight changes, institutional flow data, and Q2 margin/pricing commentary.

5. Nexperia / non-China supply-chain risk — real but hard to monetize via US stocks

The Nexperia disruption is externally supported: Nexperia disclosed that China imposed export restrictions on specific finished components/sub-assemblies from Nexperia China, and Dutch government materials also described company-specific Chinese export controls. For US market watch, this is an industry signal that supports second-source demand and non-China supply-chain optionality, but not a direct tradable recommendation. Treat it as a trigger to check ON, TXN, MPWR, VSH, and European/Japanese suppliers—not as proof that any one stock must rerate.
