Theme analysis

1. Passive components / MLCC specification squeeze

EP661's core idea is that AI server generation transitions can absorb high-spec MLCC capacity and crowd out standard parts. Murata is the strongest direct US-tradable candidate because it is a leading MLCC supplier and its latest official commentary says FY2026 multilayer ceramic capacitor revenue is expected to rise, driven by data-center component demand.

Investment judgment: priority research, not chase. The signal is plausible and now has one official support point from Murata, but the US-tradable instrument is OTC and thin. Before treating it as a buyable theme, confirm whether the improvement appears in segment margins, backlog/lead times, or explicit AI-server capacity allocation commentary.

Best expression today: MRAAY as small satellite only if broker quote shows acceptable bid/ask and volume. Otherwise use Murata as a signal and express AI infrastructure through more liquid names only when valuation is reasonable.

2. Aluminum / polymer capacitors

EP661 points to Nichicon and Nippon Chemi-Con as Japanese capacitor indicators. This run did not find a clean, liquid US-tradable line. Chemi-Con's official IR schedule shows FY2025 results are due 2026-05-14 JST, so the next run should verify the actual FY2025 release after publication.

Investment judgment: liquidity too thin / industry signals only. This may become an excellent Taiwan/Japan local-market research thread, but it is not yet a responsible US-market trade suggestion.

3. Mature-node / power semiconductor recovery

TXN and NXPI both reported improving year-over-year results. TXN Q1 revenue grew 19% YoY and management cited growth led by industrial and data center. NXPI Q1 revenue grew 12% YoY, with broad end-market improvement, and disclosed channel inventory of 11 weeks versus 9 weeks a year earlier.

The VSMC/NXP project is the most concrete long-term data point: the 300mm Singapore fab is planned for 130nm-40nm mixed-signal, power management and analog products, with initial production in 2027 and expected 55,000 300mm wafers/month in 2029. That supports a long-cycle demand/supply-control thesis, but not an immediate 2026 earnings catalyst.

Investment judgment: NXPI = priority research; TXN = core watch; both wait for better entry. We need confirmation that recovery is demand-led, not only price/cost pass-through or inventory restocking.

4. Fab/facility defensive names

EP661's Taiwan fab/facility names do not have clean US-tradable single-name expressions. TSM is related as the customer/capex bellwether but is not a facility contractor. TSM's April 2026 revenue was NT$410.7B, up 17.5% YoY, and Jan-Apr revenue was up 29.9% YoY, confirming AI/capex strength at the system level.

Investment judgment: monitor only in this US-tradable report. Do not force a US trade.

5. Optical / QCOM optional watch

QCOM is liquid and high quality, but the EP661 link is weak. Qualcomm's own IR framing emphasizes intelligent computing, Snapdragon, Dragonwing, licensing, connectivity, and capital returns; the current available official source did not make QCOM a clean optical/AI-infrastructure revenue play for this theme.

Investment judgment: exclude for now as an EP661-driven market-watch candidate. Revisit if a transcript or segment disclosure shows material data-center/AI-infrastructure acceleration.

Valuation and entry discipline

  • Do not chase TSM above a strong AI-rally move. Consider research entry only after a broad semiconductor pullback, a valuation reset, or a company-specific update that raises earnings power faster than price.
  • TXN needs demand proof. Better entry requires evidence that industrial and data-center growth persists while inventories stop building and free cash flow continues recovering.
  • NXPI needs channel-inventory discipline. The VSMC thesis is attractive long term, but the near-term trigger is end-market acceleration without channel inventory expanding further.
  • MRAAY is size-limited by liquidity. Any position should be a small satellite with limit orders only; no market orders in thin OTC lines.
  • Avoid ROHCY/Nichicon/Chemi-Con US OTC lines unless liquidity is verified at broker level. Poor liquidity can turn a good industry thesis into a bad execution.
  • ETFs are fallback, not thesis-pure. SMH/SOXX solve liquidity but dilute the passive-component insight into broad semiconductor beta.
