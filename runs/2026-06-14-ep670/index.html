Theme analysis

1. Passive components / MLCC / server component squeeze

EP670 makes passive components the strongest near-term evidence hunt. The key Gooaye signal is not “buy any passive stock”; it is more precise: AI server demand may be crowding high-end MLCC/power/passive capacity, spreading into adjacent part numbers, and improving pricing/mix if large ODM/EMS buyers accept increases.

Primary-source checks support the direction but not the full pricing thesis yet:

  • Murata’s FY2026 financial highlights say MLCC revenue increased across a wide range of applications, mainly servers.
  • Murata’s FY2027 forecast calls for revenue growth in capacitors and power-supply modules for servers and plans capex targeted especially toward demand expected from servers.
  • Vishay’s Q1 2026 results page and release show a cycle improvement marker: book-to-bill above 1, including passive components.

Investment translation: VSH stays the liquid US proxy; MRAAY is the closer thematic expression but remains OTC/liquidity-gated. Taiwan/Japan/Korea local tickers remain industry signals only. Upgrade requires official pricing, lead-time/allocation, and margin follow-through — not just market chatter.

2. SpaceX / SPCX and second-tier space capital rotation

EP670’s SpaceX lane is now directly US-tradable because SpaceX has listed as SPCX. SpaceX’s official pricing announcement confirms 555,555,555 shares at $135.00 and Nasdaq trading beginning 2026-06-12.

The investable question is not “is SpaceX a great story?” The better question is whether SPCX creates a cleaner, more liquid leader that pulls risk capital away from second-tier space/satellite equities. RKLB and ASTS still have their own execution milestones: Rocket Lab reported record Q1 revenue/backlog and Q2 revenue guidance, while ASTS targets 2026 network deployment with BlueBird launch milestones. Those facts argue against blindly shorting or excluding them. The dashboard should compare relative strength and fundamentals after SPCX’s first weeks of trading.

Discipline: SPCX is priority research, not a chase. Wait for the first public quarter, detailed segment economics, xAI/Starlink/Starship cash-consumption clarity, and post-IPO float/lockup behavior.

3. Anthropic / Fable export-control event and edge AI

Anthropic’s official statement confirms the Fable 5 / Mythos 5 access suspension was driven by a US government directive. That is a real platform/regulatory shock: investors may assign more value to open models, local inference, and edge-device AI if access to frontier closed models becomes less predictable.

But the correct investment mapping is conservative. This is not immediate proof that AAPL, QCOM, MU, ARM, AMD, or INTC revenue improves. Apple’s 2026 foundation-model note supports the product direction: Apple is pushing on-device and Private Cloud Compute models, privacy, and a new Siri / app intelligence architecture. For stocks, the next proof needs to be device shipments, usage, developer adoption, memory content, and margin contribution.

4. Existing TSM / AI infrastructure lane

TSM remains a core watch from EP669, not the main EP670 update. TSMC’s official May revenue was NT$416.975B, +30.1% year over year, with January-May revenue +30.0%. That keeps the AI demand lane strong. The discipline is unchanged: revenue strength is not the same as a margin/ASP/pricing-power upgrade. Wait for Q2 call commentary.

5. MU and QCOM hard catalysts

  • Micron’s next decision point is fiscal Q3 results on 2026-06-24. Watch HBM/high-capacity DRAM demand, pricing, inventories, gross margin, and AI PC memory content.
  • Qualcomm’s Investor Day on 2026-06-24 should test whether AI PC, gigawatt-scale data-center, industrial/physical AI, and 6G become financially material rather than narrative.
