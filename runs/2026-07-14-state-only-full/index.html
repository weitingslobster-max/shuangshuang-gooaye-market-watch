Theme analysis

1. TSMC revenue confirmed demand, not valuation safety

The strongest new official data point is TSMC June revenue. April, May, and June revenue now imply a very strong Q2 revenue setup, and the June YoY acceleration supports the AI/HPC demand lane from prior Gooaye states. That improves confidence in TSM as the highest-quality US-tradable anchor.

But the investment conclusion is intentionally narrower: revenue alone does not answer whether the stock is attractive after the move. The July 16 Q2 call must answer margin quality, capex intensity, advanced-packaging bottlenecks, and whether management frames AI demand as durable enough to justify the market’s expectations.

2. Power / analog / discrete lane remains proof-before-price

ADI has the cleanest new official support: Analog Devices completed its Empower Semiconductor acquisition after describing Empower as part of a high-density, grid-to-core AI power strategy. That makes ADI a better AI power-density checkpoint.

However, EP678’s more specific price and lead-time claims are still not primary-source facts. For ADI, ON, and VSH, the upgrade condition is not “AI power sounds important.” The upgrade condition is official or distributor evidence that pricing, backlog, gross margin, and free cash flow are improving together.

3. OSAT lane is about margin conversion

AMKR and ASX remain the most usable US-tradable OSAT expressions. Amkor’s prior Q2 guidance gave net sales of $1.75B–$1.85B, gross margin of 14.5%–15.5%, and 2026 capex of roughly $2.5B–$3.0B. Those are useful baselines, not automatic bullish proof.

The next checkpoint is simple: if advanced packaging and utilization are truly tightening, it should show up in margin, guide quality, and capital-return visibility. Revenue growth alone is not enough.

4. Meta / CoreWeave remains compute-economics, not “AI capex = buy”

Meta’s Q1 2026 release keeps the AI capex thesis live: the company guided 2026 capex to a very large range and continues to frame infrastructure as strategically important. That supports the idea that AI compute demand is real.

The risk is equally clear. More capex can become more depreciation if monetization lags. CRWV offers a more direct NeoCloud expression, but SEC filings keep debt, capital commitments, customer concentration, and backlog conversion as first-order risks. Therefore META is a monitored quality platform and CRWV remains small-satellite/speculative only.

5. No new Gooaye input means no new theme should be forced

Because no clean new latest Gooaye episode was incorporated, today should not invent a new thesis. The responsible work is to tighten the EP678 dashboard with official evidence and keep local-only or ambiguous claims out of US-tradable recommendations.

Valuation and entry discipline

  • Do not chase TSMC solely because June revenue was strong; that data is now public and may already be priced.
  • Prefer quality/liquidity first: TSM, ADI, META are cleaner than thin OTC or local-only expressions.
  • For cyclical or event names (ON, VSH, AMKR, ASX), wait for margin and cash-flow proof rather than narrative confirmation.
  • For CRWV and optical high-beta names, position sizing matters more than thesis elegance.
  • If the July 16 TSMC call confirms the demand story but the stock gaps hard, the disciplined action is still to wait for a better entry or use a small starter only if it fits portfolio risk.
