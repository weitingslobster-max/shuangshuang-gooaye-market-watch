Theme analysis

1. TSMC quality is confirmed, but valuation discipline still matters

The strongest primary-source evidence remains TSMC. Q2 revenue reached US$40.20B, gross margin was 67.7%, and Q3 revenue guidance is US$44.6–45.8B with 65.0–67.0% gross margin guidance. June revenue was NT$442.68B, up 67.9% YoY, and first-half revenue was NT$2.404T, up 35.6% YoY.

Investment interpretation: the AI/HPC foundry thesis remains strong. The discipline does not change: even excellent fundamentals can be a bad chase if crowded positioning or valuation compression dominates the next several sessions.

2. Analog / mature-node recovery is on deck, not proven yet

Texas Instruments’ Q2 call is scheduled for July 22 at 3:30 p.m. Central time. Because this run is at 06:00 PDT, the Q2 print is not available yet. That makes TXN a same-day trigger rather than a conclusion.

Upgrade evidence: demand-led order recovery, better utilization, inventory normalization, and margins improving because demand is real. Downgrade evidence: recovery language dominated by restocking, cost-pass-through, or still-weak industrial/auto demand.

3. STM checks the power/IDM lane tomorrow

STMicroelectronics lists Q2 2026 financial results for July 23 and its financial calendar shows the quiet period running through that date. For the EP672/EP679 power-semiconductor lane, STM is useful because it is a clean NYSE ADR and provides official industrial/auto/power commentary.

Upgrade only if guidance and margin quality improve. If results show revenue but no margin/cash-flow confirmation, keep it at priority research rather than an investable upgrade.

4. OSAT / advanced packaging has strategic support, but margin proof is still required

Amkor will report Q2 after the Nasdaq close on July 27 with a 5:00 p.m. ET call. Amkor’s Q1 showed record first-quarter revenue of $1.685B, up 27% YoY, but gross margin was 14.2%. The June 16 TSMC-Amkor 10-year partnership adds strategic support for the Arizona advanced-packaging thesis, but the investable question is still whether AI/advanced packaging demand improves margins and guidance.

AMKR is the cleanest US-listed OSAT expression. ASX remains useful as an ADR monitor. Do not upgrade either on partnership headlines alone.

5. Passive / discrete components still need Vishay proof

Vishay will report Q2 before the NYSE opens on August 5, with a 9:00 a.m. ET call. This is the cleanest US-listed checkpoint for the passive/discrete theme because many Asian passive-component leaders either lack clean US access or trade through thin OTC lines.

Upgrade only if orders convert into revenue, gross margin, free cash flow and constructive guidance. Keep MRAAY, MRAAF, and ROHCY as industry signals unless broker-level liquidity and spread are acceptable.

6. AI efficiency and capex ROI wait for Meta

Meta will report Q2 after market close on July 29 and host its call at 1:30 p.m. PT. For the EP679/EP680 AI-efficiency framework, the key test is not whether usage grows; it is whether paid usage, ad/product monetization and capex productivity improve without depreciation or inference costs eroding margin quality.

For META, GOOGL, MSFT, ADBE, CRM, NOW, SNOW, and OKTA, require paid adoption, retention, margin and capex-ROI proof together.
