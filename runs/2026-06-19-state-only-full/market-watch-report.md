## Theme analysis

### 1. AI-server power / PMIC remains the best EP671 lane

**MPWR** remains the cleanest US-listed expression because the company’s Q1 2026 commentary tied Enterprise Data growth directly to higher sales of power-management solutions for AI and server applications. **ON** remains the most important upgraded power-tree name because official Q1 language said AI data-center revenue more than doubled year over year and accelerated sequentially, with adoption across the power tree.

The discipline is important: these are **priority research** names, not automatic buy/add calls. The next upgrade requires continuing AI/server revenue growth, gross-margin durability, customer breadth, and guidance that does not look like a one-quarter pull-forward.

### 2. TXN and VSH are useful, but not pure EP671 expressions

**TXN** reported Q1 2026 revenue of about $4.83b and EPS of $1.68, supporting broad analog-cycle recovery. But the evidence is still too broad to call TXN a direct AI-server power beneficiary.

**VSH** reported Q1 2026 book-to-bill of 1.34 overall, including 1.47 for semiconductors and 1.23 for passive components. That supports improving cycle conditions, but VSH is still a mixed passive/discrete proxy rather than a pure MLCC/SP-Cap squeeze expression.

### 3. TSM quality remains strong; flow thesis is unproven

TSMC’s official May 2026 revenue was NT$416.98b, up 1.5% month over month and 30.1% year over year; January-May revenue was up 30.0% year over year. That supports the core AI-quality thesis. It does **not** by itself prove the Gooaye fund-flow/reallocation thesis. The next proof should come from fund/ETF weight changes, institutional flow data, and Q2 margin/pricing commentary.

### 4. 2026-06-24 is the next catalyst cluster

**Qualcomm** will host Investor Day on 2026-06-24, focused on AI across the connected edge and data center, including industrial/physical AI and gigawatt-scale data centers. This could upgrade QCOM from optional watch to cleaner data-center/edge-AI research, but only if the event gives measurable revenue, customers, product economics, and timing.

**Micron** will report FYQ3 2026 results on 2026-06-24. The key checks are DRAM/HBM pricing, high-capacity server memory demand, margin guidance, capex/supply discipline, and whether AI demand remains broad enough beyond one customer or one product class.
