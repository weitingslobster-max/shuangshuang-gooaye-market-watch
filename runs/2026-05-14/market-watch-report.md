## Theme analysis

### 1. Passive components / MLCC specification squeeze

Murata remains the best direct read-through. Its FY2026 materials/forecast continue to support higher capacitor demand tied to servers and data-center applications. This matches EP661’s thesis that high-spec MLCC demand may tighten mix/capacity.

Investment judgment: **priority research, not chase.** The thesis still needs proof in lead times, pricing, utilization, and margin mix. For Weiting’s US-market scope, **MRAAY** is still only a small, limit-order satellite candidate because OTC execution risk can overwhelm a good theme.

### 2. Aluminum / polymer capacitors

Nippon Chemi-Con’s official IR page now lists the **May 14, 2026 Summary of Consolidated Financial Results for the Fiscal Year Ended March 31, 2026**. This confirms the scheduled source is available and should be used as a fresh industry data point in the capacitor dashboard.

Investment judgment: **industry signal only**. Even if the Japan-local capacitor cycle improves, I still do not see a clean, liquid US-tradable expression. The right next step is to compare Chemi-Con/Nichicon margin and management commentary against Murata, not to force a US OTC trade.

### 3. Mature-node / power semiconductor recovery

TXN and NXPI both still support a “recovery watch” stance. TI’s Q1 2026 release reported revenue of **$4.83B, +19% YoY**, with growth led by industrial and data center, and Q2 revenue guidance of **$5.00B–$5.40B**. NXP’s Q1 release reported **$3.18B revenue, +12% YoY**, broad end-market improvement, and Q2 guide of **$3.35B–$3.55B**, but channel inventory increased to **11 weeks**.

Investment judgment: **NXPI = priority research; TXN = core watch; both wait for better entry.** NXPI’s long-cycle VSMC angle is cleaner thematically, but the channel-inventory build is a real yellow flag. TXN’s recovery is encouraging, but the EP661 discipline still requires separating genuine demand from restocking/cost effects.

### 4. TSM and fab/facility signals

TSMC’s April 2026 official monthly revenue was **NT$410.726B**, up **17.5% YoY**; January–April revenue was **NT$1.544829T**, up **29.9% YoY**. This keeps the AI/capex system-level signal strong.

Investment judgment: **core watch, not chase.** TSM is liquid and high quality, but it is not a direct passive-components/facility-contractor expression. Use it as a quality bellwether and potential core semiconductor candidate only when entry discipline is favorable.

### 5. QCOM optional watch

Qualcomm’s Q2 FY2026 release reported revenue of **$10.6B** and highlighted record QCT automotive revenue plus combined QCT automotive and IoT revenue growth of **20% YoY**. Management also pointed to future **Data Center and Physical AI** opportunities, with Investor Day scheduled for **2026-06-24**.

Investment judgment: **watch only.** QCOM is more interesting than yesterday for AI-adjacent optionality, but it still is not a clean EP661 passive-component/mature-node proxy. Upgrade only if Investor Day shows credible data-center revenue path, customer traction, and margin economics.
