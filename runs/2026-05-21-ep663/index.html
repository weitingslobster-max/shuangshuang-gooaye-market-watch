Theme analysis

1. Passive components / MLCC / aluminum-polymer capacitors

EP663 reinforces the strongest recurring Gooaye signal: passive-component supply discipline and possible pricing power. The investable problem is that many direct beneficiaries are Taiwan/Japan local lines, while the US-tradable expressions are imperfect. Murata remains the best thematic company evidence; MRAAY is the accessible line but not a clean liquid trading vehicle. VSH is the practical liquid proxy, but it is broader and more cyclical than the exact Gooaye MLCC/SP-Cap theme.

Evidence required before upgrading the theme: official price letters or distributor confirmations, lead-time extension, allocation/stopped-order notices, capex discipline, product-mix improvement, and margin follow-through.

2. Broadcom / Google TPU / custom AI silicon

EP663 adds a high-upside, lower-verification technology lane. Public evidence supports the broad direction: Broadcom’s official Q1 FY2026 release says AI semiconductor revenue grew strongly from custom AI accelerators and AI networking, and Anthropic officially announced a deeper Google/Broadcom TPU capacity partnership. That supports AVGO as a priority research name.

But the specific EP663 claims around code names, dual-die substrate, or exact packaging content are not yet verified by official public sources. Treat them as watchlist triggers, not as valuation inputs.

3. Old AI / Nvidia repair watch

The episode’s old-AI framing is useful risk discipline: a long-term AI infrastructure winner can still be a poor near-term entry if expectations, margins, or inventory are resetting. NVDA remains the benchmark for AI hardware demand and roadmap health, but the workflow should wait for confirmation rather than buying weakness mechanically.

4. Mature-node / analog / power recovery

TXN and NXPI remain the cleaner liquid US names for mature-node and industrial/auto recovery. EP663 does not replace the prior evidence test: recovery must be demand-led, not merely restocking or cost pass-through.

5. Market breadth and rotation

EP663 describes a market that is rotating rather than collapsing, while also warning that narrowing leadership raises risk. That means the report should avoid leverage and avoid converting every strong theme into an entry. If leadership narrows to only a few crowded names, the correct response is more selectivity, not more aggression.
