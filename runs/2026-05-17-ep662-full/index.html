Theme analysis

1. TSMC policy/fund-flow + AI foundry quality

TSMC is the cleanest US-tradable expression of the EP662 TSMC/fund-flow idea. The official company evidence is strong: Q1 2026 consolidated revenue was NT$1,134.10B, net income was NT$572.48B, gross margin was 66.2%, and management guided Q2 revenue to US$39.0–$40.2B. April revenue remained up 17.5% YoY, and January–April revenue rose 29.9% YoY.

Investment interpretation: TSM is quality, but quality is not enough for an entry. EP662’s policy angle is an incremental fund-flow catalyst. It becomes investable only if fund/ETF holdings and institutional net buying actually move, and if TSM’s ADR valuation still leaves margin of safety.

2. Passive components / MLCC / SP-Cap

This is the most important research change from EP662. The official evidence is not yet “SP-Cap prices are up 30% everywhere,” but it is meaningfully supportive: Murata projects FY2026 capacitor revenue growth and explicitly ties MLCC growth to data-center component demand. Murata also expects power-supply revenue for server applications to rise.

US execution hierarchy:

  • Best thematic fit: MRAAY / Murata ADR, but OTC liquidity is the constraint.
  • Best liquid proxy: VSH, because it is NYSE-listed and directly exposed to passive electronic components and discrete semiconductors.
  • Signal only: ROHCY/ROHM, Panasonic/PCRFY-like lines, Nichicon/Chemi-Con OTC lines unless liquidity is proven.

The next hard evidence should be official price letters, distributor lead-time/allocation data, company order commentary, book-to-bill, and gross-margin improvement.

3. Mature-node / analog / power recovery

NXPI and TXN remain better US-tradable expressions than most Taiwan/Japan local names. NXP’s Q1 results show broad-based improvement and a stronger Q2 outlook, with Industrial & IoT and Automotive especially relevant to EP662’s physical-AI/power-component discussion. TI’s Q1 results show analog-cycle recovery led by industrial and data center.

The key investment test: recovery must be demand-led, not just restocking or cost-pass-through. Watch channel inventory weeks, gross margin, order trends, and Q2 guidance/actuals.

4. Right-side software / cybersecurity

EP662’s software/security bucket is best treated as a momentum-quality screen. Figma has the freshest official evidence, with Q1 2026 revenue growth of 46% YoY and raised guidance. CrowdStrike’s available official Q1 FY2026 results are older but still show scale: revenue up 20% and ARR up 22% as of April 30, 2025. That supports the “strong can stay strong” thesis, but it is not enough to ignore valuation.

The right stance is watch-only until: (1) current earnings refresh confirms acceleration, (2) breadth across PANW/ZS/OKTA/NET improves, and (3) valuation leaves room for error.

5. Qualcomm optional AI infrastructure watch

QCOM is not a clean passive/power-component proxy, but it is worth keeping on the optional list. Qualcomm’s Q2 FY2026 release says data-center custom silicon is on track for initial shipments later in 2026 and that Data Center / Physical AI will be updated at Investor Day on 2026-06-24. Until then, QCOM is an event-driven optional watch, not a main EP662 expression.
