Theme analysis

1. AI-server power / MOSFET / PMIC remains the highest-priority lane

The core Gooaye-derived thesis is unchanged: AI servers can shift bottlenecks toward power delivery, high-voltage conversion, MOSFETs, PMICs, discretes, and mature-node capacity. ON and MPWR remain the cleanest US-listed proof points from public company materials. STM is increasingly relevant because management has highlighted data-center power ambitions, while TXN, UMC, and VSH are useful but less pure expressions.

The gate is still evidence quality. A shortage story becomes investable only if multiple sources show lead-time stretch, allocation, price increases, and conversion into revenue or gross margin. Without that, the right posture is priority research rather than buy/add.

2. Qualcomm Investor Day: QCOM becomes a real watchlist name

Qualcomm used its 2026 Investor Day to frame FY2029 targets around non-handset growth, with Automotive and IoT as major pillars and explicit discussion of data center and Physical AI. The company also showed a five-year capital-return target framework. This materially improves QCOM's fit as a US-tradable expression for edge AI, on-device AI, robotics/Physical AI, and potentially data-center inference.

The discipline: the targets are long-dated. The right label is priority research / no chase, not core holding. Upgrade only if future filings and segment disclosures show measurable revenue, margin, customer adoption, and durable attach rates outside handsets.

3. Micron FYQ3: AI memory proof improved, but cyclicality remains

Micron's FYQ3 materials showed strong data-center momentum, with data-center revenue setting a record, HBM revenue growing strongly, and NAND data-center SSD revenue at a record. This supports the broader AI infrastructure breadth thesis and helps the local-AI / high-capacity memory lane.

The caution is classic memory-cycle discipline: good AI demand can coexist with inventory, pricing, capex, and expectation risk. MU remains a priority research name, not an automatic add.

4. Passive/discrete and mature-node proxies still need conversion proof

VSH remains a liquid but imperfect passive/discrete proxy. UMC remains a mature-node utilization monitor rather than a direct power-semiconductor thesis. Both are useful for triangulating a cycle, but neither should be promoted without product-mix, margin, and demand-specific proof.
