Theme analysis

AI-server power / MOSFET / PMIC

The thesis remains plausible and important: AI servers increase power density and can stress analog, discrete, PMIC, and high-voltage power components. Public evidence is strongest at ON and MPWR. ON’s Q1 2026 release explicitly says AI data-center revenue more than doubled year over year and grew more than 30% sequentially, with broader adoption across the power tree. MPWR’s Q1 commentary reported Enterprise Data revenue of $262.8M, up 97.7% year over year, driven by power-management solutions for AI and server applications.

What is still missing: hard public confirmation that the specific MOSFET/PMIC/high-voltage server parts are in broad allocation, that lead times are 52–99+ weeks across multiple parts/customers, and that price increases are converting into margins. Until that appears, this remains a priority evidence hunt rather than a buy signal.

STM / IDM power-semiconductor follow-through

STM deserves attention because it is a clean NYSE-tradable IDM expression. Its April Q1 release already pointed to AI infrastructure upside; a June 2026 filing raised the 2026 data-center revenue ambition to about $1B, compared with prior language of “nicely above $500M.” That is material enough to keep STM high on the dashboard. Still, data-center revenue ambition is not the same as verified power-component shortage pricing. The next proof points are order conversion, margin durability, and explicit power/AI infrastructure content.

UMC / mature-node capacity

UMC remains the capacity monitor, not the conclusion. Monthly sales and utilization can improve for many reasons. For EP672’s thesis, the decisive evidence would be power/PMIC/MOSFET-driven mature-node demand, second-source qualification, and margin/product-mix improvement. Without that, UMC is useful as a cycle indicator but should not be promoted into a high-conviction power-semiconductor play.

QCOM and MU dated catalysts

The next two calendar checks are both on 2026-06-24. Qualcomm’s Investor Day should be judged on whether data-center / Physical AI becomes measurable rather than narrative. Micron’s FYQ3 result should be judged on HBM/DRAM pricing, capex discipline, inventory, and whether AI memory strength broadens without overheated expectations.
