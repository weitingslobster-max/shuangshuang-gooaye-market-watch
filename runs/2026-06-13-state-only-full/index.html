## Theme analysis

### 1. TSMC pricing power / AI demand

TSMC's May revenue print is the cleanest official evidence in the current dashboard. The data supports durable AI/HPC demand, but revenue alone does not prove pricing power, gross-margin durability, or customer willingness to absorb higher wafer prices. The correct stance is `核心觀察 / core watch` plus `等待更好價格 / wait for better entry`.

### 2. Apple onscreen / visual-intelligence lane

Apple's official iOS 26 materials support EP669's observation that visual intelligence can move from camera-only novelty toward screen/context actions across apps, and the Foundation Models framework gives developers a privacy/on-device path. This is meaningful product evidence, not yet investment proof. Upgrade only if adoption improves device/services economics or developer workflows in measurable ways.

### 3. AI infrastructure / CPO / 800VDC risk queue

NVIDIA/AVGO/MRVL/TSM remain the key US-tradable infrastructure lane. EP669's caution is still useful: CPO, photonics, and power architecture timing can shift. Treat roadmap changes as risk controls, not automatic buy/sell signals.

### 4. MU and QCOM event lane

Both hard catalysts remain on 2026-06-24. MU needs pricing, inventory, HBM/high-capacity demand, and gross-margin evidence. QCOM needs Investor Day disclosures that make AI PC / data-center / Physical AI financially material rather than optional narrative.

### 5. Passive, power, and mature-node components

No upgrade without hard evidence: official price letters, distributor lead-time/allocation data, or supplier margin follow-through. VSH stays the liquid US proxy; MRAAY stays the closest Murata/MLCC ADR but is OTC/liquidity-gated.
