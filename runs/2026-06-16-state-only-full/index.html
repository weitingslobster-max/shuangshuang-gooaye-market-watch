Theme analysis

1. Passive components / MLCC / server component squeeze

This remains the highest-priority evidence hunt. The useful update today is that official data continues to support a component-cycle recovery: Vishay reported Q1 2026 book-to-bill of 1.34 overall and 1.23 for passive components, with backlog at 5.7 months. That is constructive for VSH as a liquid proxy.

But it is not enough to turn EP670’s MLCC/specification-squeeze idea into a buy call. VSH is broader passive/discrete exposure, not a pure high-capacitance MLCC/SP-Cap expression. Murata is closer thematically: its FY2025 results/forecast and segment disclosures point to stronger data-center-related capacitor demand, including MLCC server revenue improvement. The US-tradable problem remains unchanged: MRAAY is OTC and must stay tiny/liquidity-gated, while MRAAF/ROHCY remain signal-only unless broker-level liquidity proves otherwise.

Upgrade path: official price letters, distributor lead-time/allocation evidence, stopped-order notices, and margin follow-through across Murata/Vishay and industry peers.

2. SPCX / SpaceX and capital rotation

SpaceX is now the cleanest US-tradable expression of the EP670 space theme. Official SpaceX/SEC/Nasdaq materials confirm the IPO pricing at $135, the sale of 555,555,555 Class A shares, and trading on Nasdaq / Nasdaq Texas under SPCX beginning 2026-06-12. Secondary reports indicate a strong first-day pop, which is exactly why discipline matters.

This validates the dashboard item but does not validate an entry price. The first public weeks carry unusually high risks: IPO allocation effects, float/lock-up mechanics, retail enthusiasm, limited post-listing disclosure history, and valuation that may already capitalize many years of Starlink/Starship/AI optionality. Keep SPCX = priority research / do not chase. Use RKLB and ASTS as relative-strength and independent-catalyst checks, not automatic losers.

3. Edge AI / model-control narrative

The Anthropic/Fable lane remains a narrative watch. For AAPL, QCOM, MU, ARM, AMD, and INTC, upgrades need measurable proof: device shipment attach rates, developer adoption, local inference usage, memory-content uplift, or paid workflows. The next two hard tests are now close: Micron FYQ3 2026 and Qualcomm Investor Day, both scheduled for 2026-06-24.

4. TSM and AI infrastructure

TSM remains the cleanest US-tradable foundry watch. TSMC’s official 2026 monthly revenue page supports a strong demand lane, with listed year-to-date consolidated revenue through the available months up sharply year over year. Still, revenue strength is not the same as margin/pricing proof. Upgrade only if Q2 commentary supports durable pricing power, gross margin, capex discipline, and customer-demand durability.

5. Software AI repair and AI interconnect

The software repair lane remains attractive but valuation-sensitive. The healthiest thesis requires proof that AI improves paid usage, retention, workflow depth, RPO/ARR quality, and margins. In infrastructure, MRVL/AVGO/NVDA remain valid AI buildout monitors, but expectations are hot; use official earnings/guidance and customer-concentration disclosures rather than momentum alone.
