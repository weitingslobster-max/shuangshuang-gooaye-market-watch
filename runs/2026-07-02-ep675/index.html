Theme analysis

1. TSMC: market anchor, but not a blank check

EP675 puts TSMC at the center of the next market test: after 6/30 rebalance and performance-window pressure, does money return to the heavyweight and validate the rally? The clean US expression is TSM.

Primary-source support remains constructive but expectation-sensitive. TSMC’s official monthly revenue page shows strong 2026 growth through May; June revenue was not yet available at the 2026-07-02 run time. Prior Q1 materials and management commentary support the AI/HPC demand story, but the next call must prove gross margin resilience amid high capex and overseas fab cost.

Stance: 核心觀察 / wait for better entry. Upgrade only if Q2 results/guidance confirm AI/HPC demand, gross margin and capex discipline. Downgrade if the result merely meets high expectations and the stock sells off with weak breadth.

2. Passive components: price letters must become financial evidence

EP675 keeps passive components in the main narrative but also warns that the story is moving into verification. July price letters and revenue/margin conversion now matter more than rumor.

US access is the problem. VSH is liquid and relevant to passive/discrete components, but not a pure Taiwanese R-chip or MLCC expression. MRAAY is a better Murata/passive thematic fit but OTC liquidity and spread risk matter. Taiwan/Japan local names remain supply-chain signals only.

Stance: 優先研究 / evidence-gated. No upgrade until at least two checks line up: price-letter confirmation, monthly revenue acceleration, gross-margin improvement, and order/lead-time support.

3. Power components / MOSFET: the externality hypothesis is plausible, not proven

EP675’s power-component lane says high-end AI demand may consume priority IDM capacity and create external demand for lower-tier or second-source suppliers. The US-tradable expressions are ON, MPWR, STM, TXN and UMC.

The hypothesis is investable only if official results and channel evidence show product-specific shortage/pricing. ST’s Q1 2026 results show revenue and margin improvement, but that is not enough by itself to prove MOSFET/AI-server lead-time squeeze. UMC’s May 2026 monthly sales were up year over year, but June was scheduled after this run; it must prove mix/utilization, not only revenue.

Stance: 優先研究 / no chase. Keep ON/MPWR/STM/TXN/UMC on the dashboard; require lead-time, allocation, pricing and margin conversion before any upgrade.

4. ON + SYNA: strategic Physical-AI angle with deal risk

The most concrete new US event is onsemi’s June 25, 2026 agreement to acquire Synaptics in an all-stock transaction. Management frames the combination as expanding from power and sensing toward Physical AI: power, sense, connected compute and control.

This fits the EP675 power/edge-AI conversation, but the investment read-through is mixed. A larger Physical-AI TAM does not automatically solve ON’s cyclical auto/industrial exposure or integration risk. SYNA should be treated as an event-driven transaction monitor, not a fundamental long-term position unless separately researched.

Stance: ON stays 優先研究; SYNA added as event monitor / transaction-risk only.

5. IC design: demand-driven beats cost-pass-through

EP675 separates good IC-design setup from low-quality cost-pass-through. MediaTek, GUC and Alchip are Taiwan-market signals, not clean US-tradable recommendations. For US accounts, QCOM, AVGO, MRVL, NXPI and maybe SMH/SOXX are cleaner expressions depending on which part of the IC-design / custom-silicon / edge-AI thesis Weiting wants.

Stance: do not buy a US proxy just because a Taiwan local ticker moved. Require direct revenue, margin and customer proof in the US-listed company.
