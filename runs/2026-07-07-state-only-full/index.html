Theme analysis

1. OSAT / advanced packaging pricing power

The thesis is directionally alive but not yet proven as a buy signal. Amkor reported record Q1 revenue and management highlighted advanced packaging programs, factory utilization, and margin initiatives. The key nuance: Q1 gross margin improved year-over-year but fell sequentially from Q4, so the next proof point is Q2/Q3 conversion, not the Q1 headline alone.

TSMC and Amkor also announced a long-term US advanced-packaging partnership in June, which improves strategic relevance for AMKR. For ASX, wait for official monthly/quarterly evidence before treating the EP676 quote-up narrative as confirmed.

Decision: keep ASX and AMKR as priority research, but do not chase. Upgrade only if pricing/mix shows up in margins.

2. Power / passive / mature-node spillover

VSH is the cleanest listed evidence point today. Vishay’s Q1 2026 release reported revenue growth, gross margin improvement, and book-to-bill above 1 for both semiconductors and passives. That supports the idea that demand is improving beyond pure AI GPU names.

But EP676/EP675 price-letter and lead-time claims still need hard confirmation. For MRAAY/MRAAF/ROHCY, liquidity remains too thin for normal-sized US-market implementation. Treat them as industry signals unless broker-level liquidity is acceptable.

Decision: VSH stays priority research; MRAAY remains small satellite only; MRAAF/ROHCY signal-only.

3. onsemi / Synaptics physical-AI event

On June 25, onsemi and Synaptics announced an all-stock transaction with an enterprise value around $7B. Strategically, it broadens ON from power/sensing toward connected compute/control for physical AI. The risk is that this changes ON from a relatively clean power-cycle monitor into an integration and deal-spread event.

Decision: keep ON in priority research but require deal-path clarity, guidance stability, and no integration shock. SYNA is event monitor only.

4. Meta / CoreWeave NeoCloud validation

Meta’s Q1 2026 result supports the demand side: Q1 revenue was strong and 2026 capex guidance rose to $125–145B. CoreWeave reported Q1 2026 revenue backlog of $99.4B and disclosed multiple new Meta agreements including a $21B commitment.

The risk side is equally large: CoreWeave still reported a GAAP net loss and high interest expense. This lane validates AI-infrastructure demand, but it is not a low-risk buy signal.

Decision: META is a priority monitor for capex monetization; CRWV is high-beta and financing-sensitive.

5. TSMC anchor

TSMC remains the highest-quality semiconductor anchor. The next two dates matter more than daily market noise: June monthly sales scheduled for 2026-07-10 13:30 Asia/Taipei and Q2 earnings call scheduled for 2026-07-16 14:00 Asia/Taipei / 02:00 ET. Current Q1/Q2 materials show very strong margins and AI/HPC mix, but expectations are high.

Decision: stay core watch; require price/volume reaction and margin guide discipline before any upgrade.
