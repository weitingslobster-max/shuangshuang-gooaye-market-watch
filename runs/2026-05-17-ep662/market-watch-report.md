## Material-change analysis

### 1. TSMC policy/fund-flow signal

EP662 adds a confirmed policy/fund-flow question: if Taiwan domestic funds can hold more TSMC, TSM/2330 may absorb capital from smaller names. For a US investor, **TSM is the cleanest expression**, but the signal is not yet the same as a buy signal. The next proof is not “policy exists”; it is **actual fund/ETF holdings, institutional net buying, and whether valuation still offers margin of safety**.

### 2. Passive components and SP-Cap / power components

EP662 makes passive/power components the highest-priority evidence hunt. The right discipline is to separate:

- **hard evidence:** official company commentary, price letters, distributor lead times, order allocation/stopped orders;
- **soft evidence:** podcast/supply-chain chatter, secondary-note guesses, one-off spot price moves.

US execution remains constrained. **MRAAY** is thematically closest but OTC-thin. **VSH** is liquid and US-listed, but it is a broader passive/discrete proxy, not a pure MLCC or SP-Cap play.

### 3. Mature-node / analog recovery

TXN and NXPI remain the best liquid US names for mature-node monitoring. EP662’s power-component discussion supports watching them, but the investment test is unchanged: revenue growth must be paired with margin and inventory discipline. TXN’s Q1 release reported revenue growth led by industrial and data center; NXPI’s Q1 release reported broad-based end-market improvement, especially industrial and automotive processing. Both still need Q2 confirmation.

### 4. Right-side software / cybersecurity

EP662’s CRWD/Figma comments add a US software watch bucket. The workflow should treat this as a **momentum/quality screen**, not a blanket software call. CRWD’s latest Stooq snapshot shows strong price action; FIG has very high trading volume but still needs post-listing evidence depth. Avoid leveraged ETF shortcuts unless explicitly requested and separately risk-reviewed.
