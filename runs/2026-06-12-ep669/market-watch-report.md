## Theme analysis

### 1. TSMC pricing power: cleanest EP669 fundamental line, still evidence-gated

EP669’s highest-quality investable theme is not a broad Taiwan-stock chase; it is the possibility that TSMC’s capacity, advanced-node demand, and pricing discipline support long-term margin and EPS expectations. Official TSMC sources confirm strong early-2026 revenue momentum through April and provide the next venue for checking gross margin and capex discipline, but the specific “price increase” framing remains unconfirmed until official company or customer evidence appears.

**Investment judgment:** keep TSM as `核心觀察 / core watch`; do not chase. Upgrade only if monthly revenue, Q2 call commentary, and margin guidance support durable pricing power without customer-demand deterioration.

### 2. Apple onscreen context: product evidence exists; supply-chain evidence does not yet

Apple’s official iOS 26 and accessibility materials support the EP669 point that visual intelligence / onscreen context is a real product direction. This matters because it moves Apple Intelligence closer to practical workflow/interface utility, especially through on-device models, Shortcuts, visual intelligence, VoiceOver/Magnifier, and natural-language control.

**Investment judgment:** AAPL is now an optional AI-interface watch, not a component-chain buy signal. Upgrade only if beta feedback, iPhone cycle data, services engagement, or developer adoption shows measurable value. Do not infer PMIC/optical supplier upside without revenue/order evidence.

### 3. CPO / 800VDC / Rubin: reality check, not thesis death

EP669 warns that SemiAnalysis-style CPO/800VDC timing concerns can trigger sharp valuation resets. NVIDIA’s official Rubin updates, however, describe Vera Rubin and Spectrum-X Ethernet Photonics as production/ramp items. That supports the view that the AI-infrastructure trend remains alive, while leaving room for timing, design, and supplier-specific disappointment.

**Investment judgment:** keep NVDA/AVGO/MRVL/TSM in `不追高 / do not chase`. MRVL is the most direct US-tradable optical/interconnect watch after Marvell’s Q1 FY2027 materials pointed to strong data-center and interconnect growth, but it still needs margin/customer concentration proof.

### 4. Quality/liquidity rotation: translate Taiwan local signals into US-tradable discipline

EP669’s “old famous names with liquidity and numbers” maps best to US-tradable quality/liquidity filters: TSM, AAPL, NVDA, AVGO, MRVL, MU, QCOM, and selective profitable software. It does **not** justify buying local Taiwan/Japan/Korea names inside this US-only workflow.

**Investment judgment:** use the style lesson as a filter: prefer names with official revenue, earnings, guidance, and liquid trading access; avoid dream-stock extrapolation without revenue visibility.

### 5. Existing catalyst lane: MU and QCOM stay unchanged

Micron has an official FYQ3 earnings call scheduled for 2026-06-24. Qualcomm has Investor Day on the same date. EP669 does not change the evidence required: MU needs memory pricing/HBM/inventory/margin proof; QCOM needs material AI PC, data-center, or Physical AI economics.

**Investment judgment:** both remain `優先研究 / priority research`, but action should wait for the event evidence.
