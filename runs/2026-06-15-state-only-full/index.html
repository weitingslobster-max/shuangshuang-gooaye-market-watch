Theme analysis

1. Passive components / MLCC / server component squeeze

This remains the strongest near-term evidence hunt from EP670. The investable question is still narrow: are AI server platforms and higher-capacitance specs producing genuine allocation, lead-time extension, pricing power, and margin follow-through?

The answer is not proven enough for a buy call. Murata remains the best thematic industry signal, and MRAAY is the closest US-accessible ADR expression, but OTC liquidity makes it unsuitable for anything beyond tiny, broker-verified satellite sizing. VSH is easier to trade but is a broader passive/discrete proxy, not a pure MLCC thesis. Upgrade only if official price letters, distributor allocation/lead-time evidence, and company margin commentary line up.

2. SPCX / SpaceX and capital rotation

EP670’s space thesis stays relevant because a public SpaceX line creates a cleaner leader expression. The temptation will be to treat SPCX as “the obvious winner.” That is too early. New public listings can combine strong business quality with poor entry timing, thin early disclosure, float constraints, and lockup-driven volatility.

The right posture is priority research / no chase. RKLB and ASTS should be measured against SPCX on relative strength and independent catalysts, not mechanically demoted. If RKLB or ASTS hold relative strength while executing milestones, the capital-rotation risk is less severe. If they weaken persistently while SPCX absorbs attention, the EP670 rotation warning gains weight.

3. Edge AI / model access restriction narrative

The Anthropic/Fable episode supports the idea that enterprises and developers may value local models, open models, and device-side inference more when frontier closed-model access is politically or operationally uncertain. But this is still narrative support, not stock-specific revenue proof.

AAPL, QCOM, MU, ARM, AMD, and INTC should remain on the dashboard, but upgrades require measurable evidence: device shipment attach rates, developer adoption, local inference usage, memory-content uplift, paid workflows, or explicit revenue disclosure. The 2026-06-24 MU and QCOM events are the next places to look for proof.

4. TSM and AI infrastructure

TSM remains the cleanest US-tradable foundry watch, but today does not change the proof gate. Revenue strength supports the AI-demand lane; it does not automatically prove durable pricing power or margin expansion. The next upgrade requires management commentary on pricing, gross margin, capex, customer mix, and demand durability.

5. Software AI repair

The software repair lane remains attractive as a research category but still valuation-sensitive. The healthiest version of the thesis requires evidence that AI improves paid usage, retention, workflow depth, RPO/ARR quality, and margins. Broad exposure through IGV may be preferable when single-name conviction is weak.
