Theme analysis

1. Data-center interconnect / optical / CPO

EP667’s strongest new US-tradable line is MRVL. Marvell’s official Q1 FY2027 release reported record net revenue of $2.418B, up 28% YoY, and guided Q2 revenue to about $2.7B. Management attributed expected acceleration to data-center strength and cited demand across 800G/1.6T scale-out optics, Ethernet switches, NPO/CPO, data-center interconnect modules, and custom XPU/XPU-attach solutions.

**Investment judgment:** this is enough to promote MRVL to priority research, but not enough to chase. Host stance in EP667 was explicitly cautious on the short-term move. The family thesis should require continuing proof in bookings, AI/custom-silicon revenue, gross margin, customer concentration, and capex durability.

2. Custom ASIC / AI networking

Broadcom’s Q2 FY2026 result was the key same-day trigger from the prior state. The company release reported Q2 AI semiconductor revenue of $10.8B, +143% YoY, driven by custom AI accelerators and AI networking. This supports EP663/EP667’s Broadcom/Google TPU/custom ASIC lane.

**Investment judgment:** AVGO stays priority research. The thesis is strong, but the stock can fall on merely “good” numbers when expectations are priced for perfection. Treat pullbacks after solid primary-source confirmation as research opportunities, not automatic buys.

3. AI PC / local-agent hardware

NVIDIA’s June 1 DGX Station for Windows announcement supports EP667’s local-agent / deskside AI hardware direction: GB300-class Grace Blackwell infrastructure is being brought into Windows enterprise workflows, with local frontier-model capability. NVIDIA’s May 31 Vera Rubin update also supports the idea that AI factory infrastructure is still scaling.

**Investment judgment:** AI PC is a second challenge, not a proven replacement cycle. For stocks, prefer infrastructure beneficiaries (NVDA/MRVL/AVGO/TSM) until OEM shipment, ASP, enterprise use-case, and software attach data are available.

4. TSMC core / Taiwan fund-flow

TSMC’s official 2026 monthly revenue page shows April consolidated net revenue of NT$410.726B, +17.5% YoY, and Jan–Apr revenue of NT$1.545T, +29.9% YoY. This supports the AI foundry core thesis. The Gooaye fund-flow catalyst remains less verified: until fund prospectus amendments and actual net buying appear, keep it as a trigger, not a thesis pillar.

5. Power semiconductors and power/analog components

EP667’s “AI power periphery” lane has initial primary-source support. onsemi’s Q1 2026 release said demand strengthened, the business moved beyond the cyclical trough, and AI data-center business grew more than 30% sequentially. ADI’s Q2 FY2026 release reported $3.62B revenue, +37% YoY, record bookings across Industrial/Automotive/Communications, and strength led by Industrial and Communications.

**Investment judgment:** promote ON and ADI to the active dashboard, but avoid saying “全面缺貨.” The upgrade trigger is sustained bookings, margin recovery, data-center power design wins, and healthier inventory/lead-time evidence.
